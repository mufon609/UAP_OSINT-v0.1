## Investigation question

Why does AARO — the office statutorily charged with investigating UAP, including efforts to obfuscate and manipulate public opinion about UAP — sit institutionally adjacent to IPMO, the office whose mission is foreign-directed influence and perception management, both under the same parent (OUSD(I&S)) with the same prime contractor (Sancorp), and what does that proximity mean for how UAP information reaches Congress and the public?

This decomposes into four operational sub-questions:

1. What does IPMO support?
2. Does IPMO support AARO through Sancorp?
3. What is the structural link between IPMO and AARO?
4. Why does AARO/Kirkpatrick avoid the term "non-human intelligence" in every official denial?

The fourth is not separable from the first three. The legal-scope-narrowing pattern in AARO denials is what makes the structural coupling consequential. If AARO simply denied UAP existence in legally exhaustive terms, the IPMO/AARO sister-office relationship would be administrative trivia. The fact that AARO denials carve out by origin (extraterrestrial / alien / off-world) and by technology category (non-human technology) but never engage the broader NHI category preserves a legal scope. That scope-preservation, plus the OUSD(I&S) parent's documented intervention to keep that scope un-disclosable, is what converts the structural coupling from coincidence into something operators would recognize as a deliberate institutional posture.

## Q1 — What does IPMO support?

**Stated mission per primary sources.** IPMO is the OUSD(I&S) "center of gravity" for foreign-directed influence, perception management ("deliberate conceal and selective reveal of strategic defense capabilities"), deception activities, and intelligence support to operations in the information environment (OIE). Outputs are thematic influence guidance and integrated influence strategies that flow downstream to combatant commands, services, and the Defense Intelligence Enterprise.

**IPMO PWS (FOIA 23-F-0906):**
- §1.2 Background: USD(I&S) is supported by DDI(C&SP), who is supported by the Director of IPMO for all influence and perception management related matters.
- §1.3 Objectives: IPMO develops broad thematic influence guidance focused on key adversaries, promulgates competitive influence strategies on defense issues that direct subordinate planning efforts, and fills gaps in policy/oversight/governance/integration for influence and perception management.
- §5.4 Contractor task: Assist IPMO leadership in the development and promulgation of strategy, plans, and policy for DoD influence, deception, and perception management related operations, activities, and investments.

The phrase "deception" appears explicitly in the IPMO PWS contractor scope. Sancorp is tasked with assisting IPMO leadership in developing strategy and policy for these activities.

**OSD OP-5 FY 2026 (July 7, 2025 release per PDF CreationDate).** IPMO is described as the OUSD(I&S) center of gravity for efforts to deliberately influence foreign defense and intelligence entities to behave in ways that benefit U.S. defense-related strategic interests. Stated activities: development of thematic influence guidance and integrated influence strategies; oversight of intelligence support to operations in the information environment; oversight and governance of deception activities; oversight and governance of deliberate conceal and selective reveal of strategic defense capabilities; management of designated compartmented programs.

**Not documented.** No primary source lists AARO as a consumer of IPMO products. No primary source describes IPMO as supporting domestic perception management.

**Structurally adjacent under USD(I&S) per FY 2026 OSD OP-5.** Defense Military Deception Program Office (DMDPO), OSD Red Team, Strategy Coordination Program Office (SCPO), Counterproliferation of WMD (CP/WMD) Office, Advanced Intelligence Capabilities (AIC) — see §AARO→AIC rebrand finding below — and the Law Enforcement Oversight and Compliance Directorate (added per April 21, 2023 DepSecDef memo designating OUSD(I&S) as PSA for Law Enforcement).

**IPMO has no publicly retrievable establishment instrument.** No SecDef or USD(I&S) memo establishing IPMO is available on defense.gov, media.defense.gov, or the OUSD(I&S) public site. Holly's May 27, 2022 Notre Dame memo asserts March 1, 2022 establishment per direction from SecDef and USD(I&S), but does not include or cite an underlying establishment instrument. The documented Business Justification for IPMO is the OSD OP-5 budget exhibit description plus the Notre Dame memo plus the FOIA-released PWS — but no establishment instrument analogous to the Hicks AARO memo exists in the public record.

## Q2 — Does IPMO support AARO through Sancorp?

No. Sancorp is the common node, not IPMO. Sancorp holds separate, direct prime contracts with each office:

- **IPMO direct primes:** HQ003422C0064 (Jun 2022), HQ003423C0061 (May 2023), HQ003424C0046 (May 2024)
- **AARO direct primes:** HQ003422C0094 (Sep 2022), HQ003424C0096 (Aug 2024)

These are independent contracting actions. There is no documented flow of IPMO-produced influence guidance routing through Sancorp into AARO. The correct framing: a single contractor holds simultaneous prime contracts inside an "influence and perception management" office and a "UAP investigation" office — both procured under the same parent administrative authority, by the same WHS Acquisition Directorate, in the same template environment.

## Q3 — What is the structural link between IPMO and AARO?

Eight couplings, all primary-source-documented, plus a temporal pivot that reframes the thesis as a two-track picture.

### Reporting-line transition, late July 2023 — the temporal pivot

AARO's reporting line ran through OUSD(I&S) (Moultrie) from establishment (July 15, 2022) through late July 2023. Within days of David Grusch's July 26, 2023 House Oversight testimony, Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks took direct AARO oversight, repositioning AARO Director Sean Kirkpatrick to report directly to her. The AARO Executive Council reported to Moultrie until that transition; afterward AARO operated under direct DepSecDef oversight.

**Primary anchors:**
- DefenseScoop (Brandi Vincent, August 30, 2023) — public confirmation of the reporting-chain change at the time of the AARO website launch (August 31, 2023). The same article reports that, between November 2022 and April 2023, the AARO website-launch package "moved back-and-forth between OUSD(I&S) front office and AARO every other week" — documenting the OUSD(I&S) Front Office / AARO PA friction that preceded the transition.
- Moultrie biographical record: AARO Executive Council reported to Moultrie until late July 2023.
- Kirkpatrick biographical record: Hicks took direct AARO oversight in late July 2023, repositioning Kirkpatrick to report directly to her.

**Temporal alignment:**
- 2023-07-26 — Grusch testifies under oath at the House Oversight Subcommittee.
- 2023-07-28 — Kirkpatrick LinkedIn post (in personal capacity) calls the Grusch hearing "insulting" to AARO officers; covered same day in Politico, July 29 in Fortune.
- 2023-late-July — Hicks takes direct AARO oversight; Kirkpatrick repositioned to report to DepSecDef.
- 2023-08-30 — DefenseScoop publicly reports the reporting-chain change.
- 2023-11 — OUSD(I&S) submits 33-page line-by-line UAPDA rewrite to congressional negotiators.

The reporting-chain transition occurs within days of Grusch's testimony. The November 2023 OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite is submitted *after* OUSD(I&S) has lost direct reporting authority over AARO.

**Two-track formulation of the structural-coupling thesis:**

| Layer | Status mid-2022 → late July 2023 | Status late July 2023 → present |
|---|---|---|
| Reporting line | Through OUSD(I&S) (Moultrie) | Direct to DepSecDef (Hicks; subsequent DepSecDef successors) |
| Budget exhibit | OUSD(I&S) section, OSD OP-5 | OUSD(I&S) section, OSD OP-5 (relabeled "AIC" in FY26) |
| Statutory PSA | OUSD(I&S) per Hicks establishment memo | OUSD(I&S) per same memo (no statutory change retrieved) |
| Sancorp shared prime | AARO HQ003422C0094 + IPMO HQ003422C0064 | Continuing through AARO HQ003424C0096 (Aug 2024) + IPMO HQ003424C0046 (May 2024) |
| Operational intervention | — | OUSD(I&S) submitted 33-page UAPDA rewrite November 2023 |

The thesis becomes: AARO's *reporting line* decoupled from OUSD(I&S) in late July 2023, but the *narrative-control plumbing* — shared budget exhibit, shared PSA, shared prime contractor, and OUSD(I&S)'s policy-rewrite channel into Congress — persisted. The November 2023 UAPDA rewrite was submitted by OUSD(I&S) *after* it had lost the operational handle, working through legislative-affairs rather than operational-tasking. This is a refined picture of the coupling mechanism, not a dissolution of the thesis.

## Q4 — Why does AARO/Kirkpatrick avoid the term "non-human intelligence"?

Because acknowledging NHI in the legal sense triggers statutory consequences. The Schumer-Rounds NHI definition — preserved verbatim in the Senate-passed UAPDA (July 2023), the OUSD(I&S) November 2023 rewrite, and SA 2610 (July 2024) — is **origin-agnostic**: any sentient intelligent non-human lifeform regardless of nature or ultimate origin that may be presumed responsible for unidentified anomalous phenomena or of which the Federal Government has become aware.

If AARO acknowledges NHI exists, the legal triggers under the proposed UAPDA included federal eminent domain over private NHI material, mandatory disclosure presumption to NARA, Independent Review Board jurisdiction, 25-year disclosure deadline, expanded whistleblower protections, and centralization/preservation of all Government NHI records.

AARO/DoD denials are systematically narrower than the NHI scope. Denials carve out by **origin** (extraterrestrial / alien / off-world) or by **technology category** (non-human technology, extraterrestrial exploitation). The origin-agnostic NHI category — which would cover sentient AI, cryptoterrestrial, interdimensional, time-displaced, or any other non-human intelligence whose origin is uncertain — is not engaged in any retrieved AARO/DoD official statement.

The pattern survives leadership transition (Kirkpatrick → Phillips → Kosloski) and survives across Vol I report production.

## OUSD(I&S) November 2023 UAPDA rewrite — primary-source analysis

Primary source: 33-page PDF released by Douglas Johnson via Ghost July 24, 2024.
URL: https://storage.ghost.io/c/c0/be/c0be35e5-1c72-42e1-af60-00793bc5b49d/content/files/2024/07/Pentagon-Nov-2023-proposed-revisions-to-UAPDA.pdf
Mirror: https://douglasjohnson.ghost.io/exclusive-revealed-pentagons-proposed-revisions-of-the-uap-disclosure-act-of-2023/

The document is a line-by-line markup with track-changes formatting. Nine specific structural elements (one preserved, eight changed):

1. **NHI definition preserved at §9003(13).** The Schumer-Rounds origin-agnostic NHI definition was kept verbatim. Structurally significant: OUSD(I&S) did not try to narrow NHI; they restructured the *mechanism* triggered by NHI.

2. **Inserted "confirmed" before UAP at multiple points.** Disclosure mandates only apply to "confirmed" UAP — and AARO is the entity that does the confirming.

3. **Replaced the independent UAP Records Review Board with AARO at §9005(c)(2)(H) and elsewhere.** The Review Board was the disclosure framework's independence mechanism. Replacing it with AARO routes all disclosure decisions through the office whose denials systematically avoid NHI.

4. **Added §9006(3) Senior Agency Official withdrawal authority.** Senior agency officials may withdraw records from the disclosure framework on grounds the records are not UAP-related. Single largest structural carveout in the rewrite.

5. **Restricted §9004(a)(1)(C) to "all unclassified U.S. Government, Government provided, or Government funded agency records."** Classified records exit the disclosure framework entirely.

6. **Changed "shall" to "should" in §9005(e) transmission requirements.** Transmission to NARA becomes advisory rather than mandatory.

7. **Replaced §9006(2) personal-privacy language with the broader Privacy Act of 1974 (5 U.S.C. § 552a).** Broadens privacy exemption considerably.

8. **Added intermediating layer in §9005(c)(2)(H).** Agencies serve "the Department of Defense and the Office of the Director of National Intelligence via the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office" rather than the Review Board directly.

9. **Weakened (but did not eliminate) the eminent domain provision.**

**Structural effect.** NHI survives as a statutory term, but no operational mechanism produces NHI records. AARO is the gatekeeper; AARO never uses NHI in denials; senior agency officials can withdraw records as not-UAP; the framework only applies to unclassified records anyway. Pipeline intact on paper, empty in practice.

**Adoption.** Largely incorporated into PL 118-31 / FY24 NDAA Subtitle C §§1841-1843. Per Kirkpatrick (July 17, 2024 von Rennenkampff interview): AARO/OUSD(I&S) opposition shaped the FY24 outcome; in his words, AARO "convinced Congress last year not to go down that road." Kirkpatrick separately confirmed by email to Johnson that no one from the White House, EOP, NSC, or any other higher authority called him or his boss to put pressure on him to soften his position.

## SA 2610 (Schumer-Rounds 2024 UAPDA) — fate

Primary source: Congressional Record, July 11, 2024; Senate amendment text at https://www.congress.gov/amendment/118th-congress/senate-amendment/2610/text; HASC Committee Print 2 at https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CPRT-119HPRT58246/pdf/CPRT-119HPRT58246.pdf.

**Chronology:**

- **Jul 11, 2024** — SA 2610 filed by Sen. Mike Rounds (for himself and Sen. Schumer) against S. 4638 (FY25 NDAA). Cong. Rec. pp. S4943-S4950. Ordered to lie on the table. Repeals PL 118-31 / FY24 NDAA Subtitle C; restores the original 2023 Schumer-Rounds framework including independent Review Board, NHI as legal trigger for eminent domain, 25-year disclosure presumption.
- **Jul 17, 2024** — Kirkpatrick interview with Marik von Rennenkampff (~70 minutes). Kirkpatrick discusses Pentagon opposition to UAPDA on record for the first time. States that AARO had successfully persuaded Congress against the original UAPDA framework the previous year and that the Review Board would have been duplicative.
- **Jul 23, 2024** — Sen. Gillibrand co-sponsors SA 2610.
- **Jul 24, 2024** — Douglas Johnson releases the November 2023 OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite via Ghost.
- **Jul 31, 2024** — Sen. Rounds tells Matt Laslo "I think we've got a shot at it" via the manager's package.
- **Aug 1, 2024** — Sen. Heinrich co-sponsors. Senate begins recess.
- **Sep 19, 2024** — Sen. Reed (for himself and Sen. Wicker) files S.Amdt. 3290, the Reed-Wicker manager's package incorporating 93 amendments to S. 4638. UAPDA NOT included.
- **Dec 7, 2024** — HASC and SASC release final agreement as House amendment to Senate amendment to H.R. 5009.
- **Dec 11, 2024** — House passes 281-140 (Roll 500).
- **Dec 18, 2024** — Senate passes 85-14 (Record Vote 325).
- **Dec 23, 2024** — Biden signs as PL 118-159, "Servicemember Quality of Life Improvement and National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2025."

Per HASC Committee Print 2: SA 2610 never received a vote on its merits at any stage.

**FY25 NDAA UAP-related language.** § 1687 prohibits NDAA funds for special access / restricted access UAP activities not reported to Congress by the DNI; restricts independent R&D contracts on UAP unless underlying activities are reported to congressional defense and intelligence committees. This is a fund-restriction, not a disclosure framework. The substantive UAPDA elements (independent Review Board, eminent domain, NHI as legal trigger, mandatory disclosure presumption) are not in PL 118-159.
