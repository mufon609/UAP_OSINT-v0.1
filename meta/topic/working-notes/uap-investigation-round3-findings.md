## Net new findings

### 1. Sancorp embedded-vendor profile — fully primary-source-anchored

The existing investigation flagged Sancorp Consulting as a likely embedded vendor at IPMO and AARO based on secondary attestation. This session produced primary-source-attestable evidence for **ten Sancorp contracts** via Sancorp's own published company-news page, USAspending.gov, MuckRock FOIA records, and a GAO bid-protest decision.

**Contract chain:**

| Date | Contract | Customer |

**Why this matters.** The existing investigation's institutional-adjacency thesis (Layer 1) had Sancorp positioned at IPMO and AARO via secondary sources. The contract dates are now primary-source-attestable and reveal something stronger than adjacency: **Sancorp's IPMO contract was awarded three months after IPMO's March 1, 2022 establishment; Sancorp's AARO contract was awarded 38 days after AARO's July 15, 2022 establishment.** This is not a vendor that grew into the relationship — this is a vendor positioned at both offices effectively at establishment. Same vendor, same OUSD(I&S), parallel construction confirmed at the contract level. The $30M OUSD(P) contract for OASD(HD&HA) + OASD(SP) extends Sancorp's footprint to the Space Policy office where Cummings served his joint duty assignment 2013–2014, tying the vendor to the same Pentagon corner that connects to the LeoLabs/True Anomaly/Sierra Space commercial space-domain-awareness sector documented in earlier rounds.

### 2. Premier Enterprise Solutions and Arlo Solutions — new embedded vendors at OUSD(I&S) Executive Director Support

The GAO bid-protest decision in *Sancorp Consulting, LLC* (B-422985) reveals that in FY 2024, Sancorp competed for and lost the OUSD(I&S) Executive Director Support contract under RFQ HQ003424R0178. The awards went to:

- **Premier Enterprise Solutions, LLC** (Upper Marlboro, MD)
- **Arlo Solutions, LLC** (Washington, DC)

Sancorp's protest was denied. Sancorp's quotation specifically referenced its work *"ensuring the viability and reliability of the US Defense Industrial Base"* — the verbatim mission of the OUSD(I&S) C-ADI sub-office that first appeared in FY 2025 OSD OP-5.

**Why this matters.** The existing investigation tracks Sancorp as the primary OUSD(I&S) vendor of interest. The GAO record identifies two additional embedded vendors — Premier Enterprise Solutions and Arlo Solutions — that took over the OUSD(I&S) Executive Director Support contract in FY 2024. These are uncatalogued targets. The Executive Director Support function is where cross-coordination across IPMO, AARO/AIC, C-ADI, and the other OUSD(I&S) sub-offices happens, so this is a high-leverage embedded-vendor position. The C-ADI mission language appearing in Sancorp's protest filing also dates Sancorp's awareness of C-ADI as an OUSD(I&S) line of effort to FY 2024 — consistent with the existing investigation's bracketing of C-ADI's first public appearance.

### 3. MuckRock FOIA case 23-F-0905 — Sancorp AARO contract records partial release

Robert Skvarla, Jr. filed FOIA case 23-F-0905 on May 21, 2023 for all records related to award ID HQ003422C0094 (Sancorp's AARO Support Services contract). On September 11, 2023, DoD released **34 of 125 responsive pages**. The remaining 91 pages were withheld.

Skvarla's request specifically flagged the timeline disparity: at the **April 19, 2023 Senate Armed Services Subcommittee on Emerging Threats and Capabilities hearing**, AARO Director Sean Kirkpatrick testified that AARO would need additional congressional authorization to begin counterintelligence work. But Sancorp's AARO contract had started August 22, 2022 — eight months earlier — and Sancorp's published service capabilities include counterintelligence.

**Why this matters.** Two possible resolutions, both consequential. Either (a) Sancorp was not performing CI work for AARO and its CI capability is irrelevant to the contract scope (in which case the partial-response withholding deserves explanation), or (b) AARO was already conducting CI work without congressional authorization at the time of Kirkpatrick's April 19, 2023 testimony. This is a Layer 2 (narrative-control / institutional-disclosure) finding either way, and the FOIA case is itself a primary-source URL that documents the partial-response withholding posture. The remaining 91 pages are a candidate target for further FOIA action.

### 4. Kirkpatrick UAPDA opposition — admitted on-the-record

In a 70-minute interview conducted by Marik von Rennenkampff on July 17, 2024 (the interview the existing investigation flagged as a pending retrieval target), Kirkpatrick made two on-the-record statements that authenticate the OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite document and confirm AARO's lobbying posture. Verbatim:

> "Last year we convinced Congress last year not to go down that road..."

In an email exchange with Douglas Dean Johnson on July 23, 2024, Kirkpatrick stated, on the OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite document Johnson had reported on:

> "However, the document does represent many of the objections I tried to articulate to Marik [von Rennenkampff, in the July 17, 2024 interview]."

**Why this matters.** Existing investigation framed AARO's opposition to the Schumer-Rounds UAPDA as institutional but inferential. Kirkpatrick's interview upgrades it to admitted-on-the-record fact, by AARO's own former Director. The email exchange functionally authenticates the November 2023 OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite document — an action AARO took during the same Q4 2023 / Q1 2024 institutional cluster the existing investigation has detailed nodes on. The Pentagon's UAPDA rewrite simultaneously asserted (per Kirkpatrick's interview) that no commercial-industry technologies or biologics existed to confiscate, *and* sought to relocate eminent-domain authority from a Schumer-Rounds Records Review Board to AARO itself. The internal inconsistency is now Kirkpatrick-authenticated.

### 5. Kirkpatrick / Skinwalker / 2018 SASC briefing — primary-source-anchored

The existing investigation flagged the "outside reviewer Kirkpatrick / Skinwalker / James Lacatski connection" as ⚠ secondary. This session closes that flag with primary-source attestation at three points.

**Brandon Fugal X post, May 9, 2024 — verbatim:**

> "On April 19, 2018, my team from Skinwalker Ranch was asked to provide a confidential briefing in Washington D.C. to the U.S. Senate Select Committee on Intelligence & United States Armed Services Committee. Sean Kirkpatrick was at the head of the table. It lasted 2.5 hrs."

**DoD official statement (per Liberation Times, June 22, 2024):**

> "Dr. Kirkpatrick had no involvement with Skinwalker Ranch. He attended one meeting on it on the Hill in 2018, which he has publicly acknowledged."

**Kirkpatrick self-attestation (Steven Greenstreet interview, June 2024):**

> "I attended a briefing at the request of Senate Armed Services Committee on what was at that time associated with the AATIP/AAWSAP research that was going on as an independent outside reviewer, and I gave them my opinions at that time."

**Why this matters.** AARO's first Director self-described as an "independent outside reviewer" of AATIP/AAWSAP research in 2018 — i.e., he was reviewing the legacy UAP programs led by James Lacatski four years before AARO existed. When AARO was stood up July 15, 2022 with Kirkpatrick as Director, the Pentagon was not picking institutional independence; it was picking institutional continuity from the legacy programs. Additionally, in the same Greenstreet interview, Kirkpatrick answered "no" to the questions "before AARO, did you have any interest at all in UFOs?" and "before AARO, did you perform any duties regarding UFOs or paranormal phenomena?" — answers directly contradicted by his subsequent admission to attending the 2018 SASC briefing as an outside reviewer of AATIP/AAWSAP. The denial pattern is documented; the contradiction is on the record. Fugal also produced photo metadata proving Kirkpatrick's attendance at the April 19, 2018 meeting after Kirkpatrick suggested he may have been thinking of a 2017 briefing.

The April 19, 2018 SASC briefing was joint with the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence — placing Kirkpatrick in front of both Senate committees that would later be central to AARO's statutory framework: SSCI (Heinrich-Rubio for the UAPTF) and SASC (Gillibrand for AARO).

## Why these findings change the existing investigation's posture

The Round 1–2 framing established AARO and IPMO as parallel-construction sister offices under OUSD(I&S), with Sancorp and other vendors flagged as likely embedded across both. Round 3's net new material **upgrades that framing from inferential to documentary at four specific points**:

1. **Layer 1 (institutional adjacency) → contract-level parallel construction.** Sancorp's IPMO contract date (June 2022, three months after IPMO establishment) and AARO contract date (August 22, 2022, 38 days after AARO establishment) demonstrate vendor placement at office-establishment timing, not later adjacency. Same vendor, same OUSD(I&S), positioned at both offices effectively at stand-up.

2. **Layer 2 (narrative-control infrastructure) → admitted-on-record AARO lobbying against UAPDA.** Kirkpatrick's verbatim "we convinced Congress last year not to go down that road" plus his email authentication of the OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite document make AARO's anti-disclosure legislative posture a documented fact rather than an inference from circumstantial evidence.

3. **Kirkpatrick node → institutional continuity from legacy programs, not independence.** Kirkpatrick's self-described role as an "independent outside reviewer" of AATIP/AAWSAP in 2018 — followed by AARO Directorship in 2022 — is institutional continuity from the legacy programs AARO was tasked with reviewing. The public framing of Kirkpatrick as a fresh, neutral, no-prior-UAP-involvement technical leader is contradicted by his own admission and by Fugal's primary-source evidence of his attendance at the April 19, 2018 Skinwalker Ranch briefing.

4. **Embedded-vendor surface expanded.** Premier Enterprise Solutions and Arlo Solutions enter the catalog as new OUSD(I&S) Executive Director Support contractors (FY 2024 award via RFQ HQ003424R0178). The Executive Director function is the cross-coordination layer across all OUSD(I&S) sub-offices, so these vendors have institutional reach across IPMO, AARO/AIC, C-ADI, CP/WMD, SCPO, and the LE Directorate.
