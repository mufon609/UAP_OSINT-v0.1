## Methodology

This audit answers a discipline question: every organization node in the repository should carry a documented statement of why it was created — equivalent to the Business Justification ticket attached to a network-security firewall change. The BJ has to be a real document — an establishment memo, statute, DoDD, DoDI, OSD OP-5 budget exhibit line item, or equivalent — not a narrative reconstructed from secondary reporting.

Each org section below states:

1. **Establishment instrument** — the actual document that created the office, with URL where retrievable
2. **Stated mission language** — paraphrased from primary source, with key phrases preserved verbatim where mission scope is at stake
3. **Statutory authority** — the public law cited by the establishment instrument, where applicable
4. **Predecessor and disposition** — what the office replaced, was renamed from, or has since become
5. **Gap status** — what is documented vs. what remains undocumented at primary-source level

Where prior investigator drafts wrote narrative bridging — "AARO is the latest node in a chain stretching back to Project Blue Book" or "IPMO is the latest in a long line of DoD attempts to consolidate fragmented influence functions" — those framings are NOT preserved here. The Project Blue Book lineage is at most a retrospective inventory in HRR Vol I, not a documented institutional succession. The OSI-to-IPMO continuity is constructed historiography, not institutional self-statement.

## Organization nodes

### AARO — All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office

**Establishment instrument (primary):** Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks memorandum, "Establishment of the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office," July 15, 2022. Page 1 control number OSD005853-22/CMD007521-22.
URL: https://media.defense.gov/2022/Jul/20/2003039074/-1/-1/1/ESTABLISHMENT-OF-THE-ALL-DOMAIN-ANOMALY-RESOLUTION-OFFICE.PDF

**Establishment instrument (subordinate, USD(I&S) implementation):** Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security Ronald Moultrie memorandum, "Establishing, Resourcing and Leadership of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office," July 20, 2022.
URL: https://media.defense.gov/2022/Jul/20/2003039081/-1/-1/1/ESTABLISHMENT-RESOURCING-AND-LEADERSHIP-OF-THE-ALL-DOMAIN-ANOMALY-RESOLUTION-OFFICE.PDF

**Stated mission per Hicks memo (✅ primary):** AARO synchronizes efforts across DoD and with other Federal departments and agencies to detect, identify, and attribute objects of interest in, on, or near military installations, operating areas, training areas, special-use airspace, and other areas of interest, and as necessary to mitigate associated threats to safety of operations and national security. Includes anomalous, unidentified space, airborne, submerged, and transmedium objects. The Hicks memo designates AARO as "the authoritative office of the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) and UAP-related activities for the DoD."

**Stated mission per Moultrie memo (✅ primary):** Per the Moultrie memo PDF, Moultrie articulates AARO's necessity in terms of maintaining awareness of anomalous objects in all domains, keeping pace with adversary novel-technology development and employment, and providing maximum transparency while safeguarding classified and controlled-unclassified information.

**Statutory authority (✅ primary):** Hicks memo cites NDAA FY22 § 1683 (Pub. L. 117-81, Dec. 27, 2021, 135 Stat. 2118) as the legal trigger. Codified at 50 USC § 3373. Subsequently amended by NDAA FY23 § 1673(c) and § 6802(a) (Pub. L. 117-263, Dec. 23, 2022 — the major "anomalous" rewrite and historical-record-report mandate); NDAA FY24 § 7341 (Pub. L. 118-31, Dec. 22, 2023); NDAA FY25 § 1089 (Pub. L. 118-159, Dec. 23, 2024 — counter-UAS liaison requirement).

**Predecessor (✅ primary):** AOIMSG (Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronization Group), established by Hicks memo of November 23, 2021, itself the successor to UAPTF. The Hicks AARO memo explicitly amends her own prior November 23, 2021 AOIMSG-establishment memo. AARO is therefore — at primary-source establishment-document level — a renamed and scope-expanded AOIMSG, not a successor to Project Blue Book or to AAWSAP/AATIP. AARO HRR Vol I (March 2024) describes Blue Book and AAWSAP/AATIP in its retrospective inventory of historical UAP investigations; that inventory is descriptive context, not a lineage claim.

**Director lineage:**
- Sean M. Kirkpatrick — Director, July 20, 2022 – December 1, 2023.
- Timothy A. Phillips — Acting Director, October 2023 – August 26, 2024.
- Jonathan T. Kosloski — Director, August 26, 2024 – present (on detail from NSA).

**Disposition:**
- Reporting line: through OUSD(I&S) (Moultrie) from establishment until late July 2023. ✅ Per DefenseScoop (Brandi Vincent, August 30, 2023), Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks took direct AARO oversight in late July 2023, repositioning the AARO Director to report directly to her — within days of David Grusch's July 26, 2023 House Oversight testimony. AARO has operated under direct DepSecDef reporting since.
- Operational milestone: ✅ AARO reached Full Operational Capability October 1, 2024 (start of FY 2025) — DOD spokesperson Sue Gough to DefenseScoop, December 5, 2024: AARO "now has the requisite personnel, expertise, facilities and tools to carry out its core mission."
- Public name: AARO continues to operate publicly under that name as of May 1, 2026 (aaro.mil active, Kosloski named director, Vol I accessible, Congressional/Press Products page maintained, FOIA 25-F-1218 OUSD(I&S) consolidated organization chart still lists "ALL-DOMAIN ANOMALY RESOLUTION OFFICE (AARO)").
- Budget-exhibit name: ⚠ FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (July 7, 2025 release per PDF CreationDate metadata) names the OUSD(I&S) sub-office corresponding to AARO's mission "Advanced Intelligence Capabilities (AIC)." See AIC section below.
- Caseload: per Pentagon spokesperson Sue Gough to DefenseScoop February 25, 2026, AARO has been examining over 2,000 UAP cases (caseload grew by at least 400 since the late-2024 public update of more than 1,600 reports).
- Statutory mandate execution: ⏳ HRR Vol II not yet published as of May 1, 2026. Statutory deadline per NDAA FY23 § 6802(j) was 540 days after enactment of the IAA FY 2023 (December 23, 2022) — approximately mid-June 2024. ~22 months overdue.

**Gap status:** ✅ AARO Business Justification is fully documented at establishment-document, statutory-authority, and budget-exhibit levels. The strongest documented BJ in this entire repository.

### AIC — Advanced Intelligence Capabilities (apparent FY 2025-onward OSD OP-5 budget-document name for AARO)

**Establishment instrument:** ⏳ Not separately established at any retrievable primary-source level. First appears in OSD OP-5 FY 2025 budget submission (released March 11, 2024 per PDF CreationDate metadata) as an OUSD(I&S) sub-office; retained in FY 2026 OP-5 (released July 7, 2025).
URL: https://comptroller.war.gov/Portals/45/Documents/defbudget/FY2026/budget_justification/pdfs/01_Operation_and_Maintenance/O_M_VOL_1_PART_1/OSD_OP-5.pdf

**Stated mission per FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (✅ primary):** AIC reports directly to the DEPSECDEF and the Principal Deputy Director of National Intelligence and synchronizes efforts across DoD and other Federal departments and agencies to detect, identify, and attribute objects of interest in, on, or near military installations, operating areas, training areas, special-use airspace, and other areas of interest, and as necessary to mitigate associated risks to safety of operations and national security. Includes anomalous, unidentified space, airborne, submerged, and transmedium objects.

**Critical observation:** The AIC mission language is functionally identical to AARO's Hicks-memo mission language. The reporting line — direct to DEPSECDEF and PDDNI — is the NDAA FY23 § 1683 statutory carveout language unique to AARO. No other OSD office has that statutory reporting structure. The FY 2024 OSD OP-5 (March 20, 2023 release per PDF CreationDate) named the same office "All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)" with substantively identical mission language; the FY 2025 OSD OP-5 (March 11, 2024 release per PDF CreationDate) was the first to substitute "Advanced Intelligence Capabilities (AIC)". The budget-exhibit name change is therefore most plausibly a budget-document relabeling of AARO, not a separate program.

**Three interpretations, none yet primary-source-confirmed beyond the budget submission itself:**

1. **AIC is an umbrella budget category** that includes AARO and possibly other intelligence-collection efforts under OUSD(I&S). AARO sits as the operational office under the AIC budget line. Bureaucratically routine.
2. **AIC is a quiet rebrand at the budget-document level** that has not yet been publicly announced. Consistent with the broader legal-scope-narrowing pattern documented in the structural-coupling finding.
3. **AIC is a new program that absorbed AARO**, with the same statutory carveout reporting line because AARO's legal structure was retained as the umbrella's operational basis.

**Inference flag.** Each interpretation has different policy implications. (1) is bureaucratically routine. (2) and (3) would be significant. The investigation does not currently confirm which interpretation is correct, but the budget-document-level fact — that AARO is described under a different name starting in the FY 2025 OSD OP-5 (March 11, 2024) and retained in FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (July 7, 2025) — is itself documented.

**Gap status:** ⏳ Administrative basis for the AARO→AIC budget-document description not yet retrieved. No public DoD announcement of an AARO renaming.

### IPMO — Influence and Perception Management Office

**Establishment instrument:** ⏳ Not publicly retrieved. The closest available primary source is James Holly's May 27, 2022 Notre Dame memo, which states that IPMO was established March 1, 2022 per direction from the Secretary of Defense and the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security. No actual SecDef or USD(I&S) establishment memo for IPMO is available on defense.gov, media.defense.gov, or the OUSD(I&S) public site.

**Closest available primary source — Notre Dame memo (Holly, May 27, 2022) (✅ primary):**
URL: https://omva.nd.edu/assets/474472/ipmo_nd_memo_cao_27may2022_cleared_1_.pdf

Direct paraphrase: per direction from SecDef and USD(I&S), the Influence and Perception Management Office was established on March 1, 2022. IPMO is tasked with the development of broad thematic messaging guidance and specific strategies for the execution of DoD activities designed to "influence foreign defense-related decision-makers to behave in a manner beneficial to U.S. interests." Memo signed by James A. Holly, Acting Director, IPMO.

**Stated mission per FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (✅ primary):** IPMO is the OUSD(I&S) "center of gravity" for efforts to deliberately influence foreign defense and intelligence entities to behave in ways that benefit U.S. defense-related strategic interests. IPMO integrates, matures, and operationalizes oversight, governance, and execution of cross-cutting influence activities. IPMO's stated activities include development of thematic influence guidance and integrated influence strategies; oversight of intelligence support to operations in the information environment; oversight and governance of deception activities; oversight and governance of deliberate conceal and selective reveal of strategic defense capabilities; and management of designated compartmented programs.

**⚠ Secondary-anchored claim:** Per Holly's October 3, 2023 NSI Speaker Series talk, three Secretaries of Defense across two administrations are reported to have signed off on IPMO's establishment. The NSI 23-S-3438 IPMO 101 slides have not been retrieved (original NSI URL 404; Wayback retrieval pending). The "three SecDefs across two administrations" attribution remains ⚠ secondary-anchored until the NSI primary surfaces.

Separately and earlier, Klippenstein (The Intercept, May 17, 2023) observed at primary-source level — based on his own document searches — that no public paper trail for IPMO's establishment was retrievable. That observation is itself primary-source-anchored independently of the later October 2023 "three SecDefs" claim.

**Statutory authority:** ⏳ Not cited at primary-source level. No NDAA section, no DoDD/DoDI, no public 10 USC or 50 USC code reference points to IPMO's existence. IPMO appears to exist on internal SecDef/USD(I&S) authority alone, without a statutory hook analogous to AARO's NDAA FY22 § 1683.

**Predecessor:** Not stated in any retrievable primary source. Holly's NSI Speaker Series remarks reference a two-decade institutional debate about consolidating fragmented influence-related governance, but Holly does not name a specific predecessor office, and IPMO is not formally a successor to any prior named office. Secondary reporting (Klippenstein, MintPress, Kit Klarenberg, Wikipedia) draws connections to the Bush-era Office of Strategic Influence (OSI, 2001-2002) and to subsequent Strategic Communication coordinator roles between 2002 and 2018; those connections are journalistic/historiographic, not documented institutional succession.

**Current leadership:** ⏳ Not retrieved at primary-source level post-Holly. The May 2022 Notre Dame memo identifies Holly as Acting Director. The FY 2026 OSD OP-5 does not name an IPMO Director.

**Gap status:** ⏳ IPMO Business Justification is partially documented. The OSD OP-5 budget exhibit and Holly's Notre Dame memo together provide stated mission language. The actual establishment instrument is not publicly available. This is the most consequential documentation gap in the repository's organization-node coverage. Closing it requires either FOIA scoped to OUSD(I&S) for the establishment memo, or a discovery that IPMO was established without a stand-alone instrument and instead via the OUSD(I&S) reorganization processes.

### AOIMSG — Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronization Group (predecessor to AARO)

**Establishment instrument:** ⏳ Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks memorandum, "Establishment of the Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronization Group," November 23, 2021. Cited by reference in the Hicks AARO memo of July 15, 2022. PDF not yet retrieved at primary-source level; URL pattern presumably parallel to the Hicks AARO memo at media.defense.gov.

**Stated mission per Hicks AARO memo (which references the AOIMSG memo) (✅ primary):** AOIMSG was directed to synchronize efforts across DoD and with other Federal departments and agencies to detect, identify, and attribute objects of interest in special-use airspace.

**Statutory authority:** Established before NDAA FY22 § 1683 was enacted (Dec. 27, 2021). AOIMSG's establishment was internal DepSecDef direction, not statutorily mandated. **Predecessor (✅ primary):** UAPTF (Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force), established August 4, 2020 by Deputy Secretary of Defense David Norquist memorandum and led by the Department of the Navy. UAPTF was disestablished consistent with the AARO establishment per the Hicks AARO memo's direction to the Secretary of the Navy.

**Disposition:** Renamed and scope-expanded into AARO by Hicks memo of July 15, 2022. AOIMSG existed for approximately seven months (Nov 23, 2021 — Jul 15, 2022).

**Gap status:** ⏳ Hicks AOIMSG memo of November 23, 2021 not yet retrieved at primary-source level. Should be available via the same media.defense.gov URL pattern.

### UAPTF — Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force (predecessor to AOIMSG)

**Establishment instrument:** ⏳ Deputy Secretary of Defense David Norquist memorandum, August 4, 2020. PDF not yet retrieved at primary-source level.

**Stated mission per AARO HRR Vol I (March 2024) Section IV (✅ primary):** UAPTF was established to improve understanding of and gain insight into the nature and origins of UAP. Mission was to detect, analyze, and catalog UAP that could potentially pose a threat to U.S. national security. Operated under USD(I&S) cognizance with the Department of the Navy as lead.

**Statutory authority:** Originally established without statutory mandate. Statutory grounding came retroactively via NDAA FY22 § 1683 directing establishment of an office to carry out UAPTF duties.

**Predecessor:** ⏳ No documented institutional predecessor at establishment-instrument level. AATIP/AAWSAP (DIA-managed program 2009-2012, terminated by DIA in 2012) and Project Blue Book (1947-1969) are described in HRR Vol I retrospective inventory of historical USG UAP investigations, but those are descriptive context, not institutional-succession claims. Senate Report 116-233 (Senate Select Committee on Intelligence report on the IAA FY 2021) directed ODNI in consultation with SecDef to submit an intelligence assessment on UAP; the June 2021 ODNI Preliminary Assessment followed. None of those documents names a specific predecessor office for UAPTF.

**Disposition:** Disestablished July 2022 per the Hicks AARO memo direction to the Secretary of the Navy.

**Gap status:** ⏳ Norquist UAPTF establishment memo of August 4, 2020 not yet retrieved at primary-source level.

### OUSD(I&S) — Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (parent organization)

**Establishment instrument (✅ primary):** Statutory. Originally established as USD(I) by NDAA FY03 § 901 (Pub. L. 107-314, Dec. 2, 2002 — the Bob Stump NDAA for FY 2003). Renamed to USD(I&S) by NDAA FY20 § 1621 (Pub. L. 116-92, Dec. 20, 2019).

**Charter (✅ primary):** DoD Directive 5143.01, "Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (USD(I&S))," originally issued October 24, 2014, incorporating Change 2 on April 6, 2020. Establishes USD(I&S) as the Principal Staff Assistant (PSA) and advisor to SecDef and DepSecDef regarding Intelligence, Counterintelligence, Security, sensitive activities, and other intelligence-related matters; exercises SecDef authority, direction, and control over DIA, NGA, NSA/CSS, NRO, and DCSA. Establishes the dual-hat Director of Defense Intelligence (DDI) role under which USD(I&S) reports to the DNI as principal advisor on defense intelligence matters.

**Statutory authority (✅ primary):** 10 USC § 137. Per CRS Defense Primer on USD(I&S) (Updated January 14, 2025): the Under Secretary is appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate, reports directly to the Secretary of Defense, and serves as the Principal Staff Assistant and advisor to the Secretary of Defense on intelligence, counterintelligence, security, sensitive activities, and other intelligence-related matters.

**Stated mission per FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (✅ primary):** OUSD(I&S) is the PSA and advisor to SecDef and DepSecDef regarding intelligence, counterintelligence, security, law enforcement, sensitive activities, and other intelligence-related matters. Exercises SecDef's authority, direction, and control over DoD's intelligence/CI/security/law-enforcement Defense Agencies and Field Activities. ⏳ April 21, 2023 DepSecDef memo additionally designated OUSD(I&S) as the Principal Staff Assistant for Law Enforcement (memo referenced in FY 2026 OSD OP-5 but the memo itself has not yet been retrieved at primary-source level).

**Workforce composition (✅ primary):** Per GAO-21-295 (May 6, 2021), 78 percent of OUSD(I&S) workforce is non-permanent — contractors, joint duty assignees, military/reservists, liaison officers/detailees — as of July 2020. GAO-21-295 also found that OUSD(I&S) gained new mission responsibilities every year from 2017 through 2020, including in artificial intelligence, law enforcement, personnel vetting, and identity intelligence; identified governance bodies not operating as intended and unclear roles and responsibilities. The 78% non-permanent workforce composition is the structural reason contractor support — including Sancorp's shared-prime arrangement across IPMO and AARO — is load-bearing inside OUSD(I&S), not peripheral.

**Adjacent UAP-governance decision in the USD(I)-chain record (✅ primary):** USD(I) James R. Clapper Jr. memorandum to Deputy Secretary of Defense, "Senator Reid AAITP SAP request," November 17, 2009. Verbatim signature line:
> Based on the recommendation from DIA and my staff's review of the technical reports, I recommend against establishing a Special Access Program at this time. — James R. Clapper Jr.

This is not an AARO predecessor establishment instrument — neither the Hicks AARO memo nor any subsequent AARO instrument names AAWSAP/AATIP as predecessor — but it is documented USD(I)-chain UAP-governance activity from 2009. Worth recording in the OUSD(I&S) UAP-governance record alongside the documented three-link AARO predecessor chain (UAPTF → AOIMSG → AARO).

**Recent oversight surface (✅ primary):** DoD Inspector General Management Advisory DODIG-2026-047, "Management Advisory: DoD Policy and Training on Dissemination Controls for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)," January 29, 2026. Addressed for the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security. Confirms that DoD Instruction 5200.48, "Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)," is issued by OUSD(I&S) and establishes DoD program requirements for designating, marking, handling, and decontrolling CUI. Five recommendations (two closed, three resolved-but-open). Relevant for evaluating OUSD(I&S)'s information-control posture.

**Naming convention (post-EO 14347):** Executive Order 14347 of September 5, 2025, "Restoring the United States Department of War" (Federal Register Vol. 90 No. 173, September 10, 2025) authorizes subordinate officials within the Department of Defense to use corresponding secondary titles such as Under Secretary of War in official correspondence, public communications, ceremonial contexts, and non-statutory documents. EO 14347 § 2(e) preserves: "Statutory references to the Department of Defense, Secretary of Defense, and subordinate officers and components shall remain controlling until changed subsequently by the law." OUSD(I&S) statutory designation per 10 U.S.C. § 137 remains the codified form. Hansell's official biography on war.gov uses the post-EO institutional title "Under Secretary of War for Intelligence and Security."

**Current leadership (✅ primary):** Bradley D. Hansell, sworn in July 25, 2025, after Senate confirmation 61-35 on July 22, 2025 (cloture vote July 17, 2025; Senate roll call 425). Pre-confirmation background per official USDIS bio: U.S. Navy and U.S. Army service through 2015 (~13 years total), NSC senior director under first Trump administration, Boston Consulting Group public-sector practice, Outlander VC, Operating Partner at Windage Partners. Sources: https://ousdi.defense.gov/Portals/73/2025%20Official%20USDIS%20Bio%20Hansell_Bradley.pdf; PN22-7 at https://www.congress.gov/nomination/119th-congress/22/7; cloture vote at https://www.govtrack.us/congress/votes/119-2025/s415; roll call 425 at https://www.senate.gov/legislative/LIS/roll_call_votes/vote1191/vote_119_1_00425.htm.

**USD(I&S) lineage during AARO era:** Ronald Moultrie (June 2021 – February 2024) → Milancy Harris (acting, March 2024 – ~January 2025) → Dustin Gard-Weiss (acting, January 2025 – July 2025) → Hansell (confirmed, July 25, 2025 – present as of May 1, 2026).

**Gap status:** ✅ Fully documented at statutory, charter, and OSD OP-5 budget-exhibit level. ⏳ One pending retrieval: April 21, 2023 DepSecDef memo designating OUSD(I&S) as PSA for Law Enforcement.

## Cross-cutting findings

### Finding 1: AARO's Business Justification is the strongest in the repository

AARO is the only office in this audit with a fully documented BJ at all four levels: (a) DepSecDef establishment memo, (b) USD(I&S) implementation memo, (c) statutory authority codified at 50 USC § 3373 with five enacting/amending public laws, and (d) OSD OP-5 budget exhibit line.

This bounds what can and cannot be inferred. AARO exists for a documented reason (UAP detection/identification/attribution mission, with Congress directing establishment via NDAA FY22 § 1683). The investigation does not dispute that. What the structural-coupling finding documents are residual structural and behavioral patterns that are not fully explained by AARO's stated mission alone.

### Finding 2: IPMO's Business Justification is partially documented and the establishment instrument is missing

This is the most consequential gap in the entire repository's organization-node coverage. Holly's Notre Dame memo asserts that IPMO was established March 1, 2022 per direction from SecDef and USD(I&S), but no actual SecDef or USD(I&S) memo establishing IPMO is publicly retrievable. The OSD OP-5 budget exhibit provides stated mission language but is not an establishment instrument.

Klippenstein's reporting (May 2023) explicitly noted at primary-source level that there is no public paper trail for IPMO's establishment. As of May 1, 2026, that gap remains. AARO's establishment was announced, posted to defense.gov, and accompanied by congressional briefings, press release, and a USD(I&S) implementation memo. IPMO's establishment was not publicly announced; the office existed for over a year before journalistic discovery. The asymmetry in how the two offices were stood up is itself a documented institutional pattern.

### Finding 3: AARO appears to be described as "Advanced Intelligence Capabilities" starting in the FY 2025 OSD OP-5 budget submission — three interpretations possible

The FY 2024 OSD OP-5 (released March 20, 2023 per PDF CreationDate) names the office "All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)" in the OUSD(I&S) sub-office description. The FY 2026 OSD OP-5 (released July 7, 2025 per PDF CreationDate) names the same OUSD(I&S) sub-office "Advanced Intelligence Capabilities (AIC)" using mission language and reporting-line language essentially identical to AARO's NDAA FY23 § 1683 statutory carveout. The FY 2025 OSD OP-5 (released March 11, 2024 per PDF CreationDate) was the first to name the AIC, narrowing the budget-document rename window to the FY24 → FY25 release cycle. No public DoD announcement of an AARO renaming has been issued. AARO continues to operate publicly under that name.

Three interpretations, none yet primary-source-confirmed beyond the budget submission itself: (1) AIC is an umbrella budget category that includes AARO and possibly other intelligence-collection efforts under OUSD(I&S); bureaucratically routine; (2) AIC is a quiet rebrand at the budget-document level that has not yet been publicly announced; consistent with the broader legal-scope-narrowing pattern; (3) AIC is a new program that absorbed AARO. Each interpretation has different policy implications. Significant enough to warrant its own canonical node.

### Finding 4: Five OUSD(I&S) sub-offices form a tight institutional cluster at the budget-exhibit level — distinct from reporting-line adjacency

The FY 2026 OSD OP-5 OUSD(I&S) section lists seven sub-offices in sequence: DMDPO, OSD Red Team, IPMO, SCPO, CP/WMD Office, AIC, and the Law Enforcement Oversight and Compliance Directorate. Five of these (DMDPO, OSD Red Team, IPMO, SCPO, AIC) share the most directly overlapping mission space — influence governance, adversary perspective, anomaly attribution. CP/WMD and the Law Enforcement Oversight Directorate share the OUSD(I&S) parent but operate in different mission space.

**Two-track distinction.** This adjacency is at the budget-exhibit level only. AARO/AIC's *operational reporting line* moved to the Deputy Secretary of Defense in late July 2023 per the NDAA FY23 § 1683 statutory carveout — direct to DEPSECDEF and PDDNI. The budget-exhibit adjacency (AIC listed alongside IPMO and the other sister offices in the OUSD(I&S) section) and the reporting-line adjacency (AIC reports to DepSecDef, not USD(I&S)) are distinct facts. Both are documented; neither implies the other.

The cluster of five at the budget-exhibit level is what anchors the structural-proximity claim at primary-source level.

### Finding 5: Statutory authority is asymmetric

AARO/AIC has strong statutory authority: 50 USC § 3373 with five enacting/amending public laws over four years, periodic reauthorization through NDAAs, and explicit carveout reporting line (DepSecDef + PDDNI) embedded in statute.

IPMO has no statutory authority cited at primary-source level. The Holly Notre Dame memo cites only "direction from the Secretary of Defense and Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security." No NDAA section, no codified provision, no DoDD/DoDI publicly available.

This asymmetry has policy-relevant implications. A statutorily-mandated office (AARO) has Congressional oversight handles, GAO audit access (per NDAA FY23 § 6803), and statutory reporting requirements. An internally-directed office without statutory grounding (IPMO) has none of those handles. A future administration could disestablish IPMO with a single SecDef memo; AARO would require legislative action.

### Finding 6: Predecessor chains for AARO are documented; OSI-to-IPMO continuity is not

The AARO predecessor chain is documented at establishment-instrument level: UAPTF (Norquist Aug 4, 2020 memo) → AOIMSG (Hicks Nov 23, 2021 memo) → AARO (Hicks Jul 15, 2022 memo). Three direct establishment instruments over 23 months. No earlier predecessor is named in any AARO establishment instrument.

The Clapper 2009 AAWSAP SAP rejection memo is documented USD(I)-chain UAP-governance activity but does not establish AAWSAP/AATIP as a predecessor in the institutional-succession sense. AARO HRR Vol I describes Blue Book and AAWSAP/AATIP in its retrospective inventory; that is descriptive context, not a lineage claim. The repository should not treat the Clapper memo or the HRR Vol I retrospective as predecessor succession.

By contrast, claims that IPMO is a successor to OSI (Office of Strategic Influence, 2001-2002) or to subsequent Strategic Communication coordinator roles are journalistic/historiographic, not documented institutional succession. The Holly Notre Dame memo names no predecessor. The FY 2026 OSD OP-5 names no predecessor. The repository should not assert OSI-to-IPMO succession as if it were institutionally documented.
