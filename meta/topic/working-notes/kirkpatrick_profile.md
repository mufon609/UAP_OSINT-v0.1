# Sean M. Kirkpatrick: A Professional Profile

*Compiled from primary sources, government documents, contemporaneous journalism, and corporate records. Last revised April 28, 2026.*

---

## Executive Summary

Dr. Sean Michael Kirkpatrick (born 1967/1968) is an American laser and materials physicist who served as the inaugural director of the U.S. Department of Defense's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) from July 20, 2022 through December 1, 2023. By his own and his employers' accounts, AARO was the sixth Intelligence Community or Department of Defense "new-start" organization for which he served as the IC or DoD lead in establishment — the culmination of a twenty-seven-year career spanning the Naval Research Laboratory, the Air Force Research Laboratory, the National Reconnaissance Office, the CIA's Directorate of Science and Technology, the Defense Intelligence Agency, the Office of the Secretary of Defense, the National Security Council, U.S. Strategic Command, and U.S. Space Command.[^1][^2][^4]

This profile documents Kirkpatrick's full career, his AARO tenure, his patent portfolio, and his post-AARO multi-channel posture across the Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Elara Nova: The Space Consultancy, WestExec Advisors, and his own consultancy Nonlinear Solutions LLC. Particular attention is given to the **October 2023 timeline**, during which Kirkpatrick simultaneously orchestrated AARO's institutional defense against legislative reform efforts while initiating a multi-channel post-government landing — a pattern that has substantial implications for federal ethics rules governing employment negotiations and post-government-employment financial disclosure.

The bigger picture this profile assembles is one in which Kirkpatrick functioned, by his own contemporaneous self-description, as an institutional **defender** of an architecture larger than himself: an Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (OUSD(I&S))–anchored apparatus engaged in what statutory law and current OSD budget materials describe as "deliberate conceal and selective reveal of strategic defense capabilities."[^60][^61] Kirkpatrick is not the principal subject of that architecture; he is one identifiable mid-level node within it. But he is the most documented, the most public-facing, and — given the volume of primary-source material now available — the most legible.

## Part I — Personal Background and Education

### Origins

Sean Michael Kirkpatrick was born in 1967 or 1968 in Columbus, Georgia, to an Army family.[^2] He was raised primarily in **Duluth, Georgia**, an Atlanta suburb in Gwinnett County.[^2][^3]

### The 1986 Brookhaven appointment — a foundational Department of Energy tie

In 1986, at age eighteen, Kirkpatrick was selected as a member of the U.S. Department of Energy's High School Honors Research Program at **Brookhaven National Laboratory** on Long Island, New York.[^2] Per the Wikipedia entry's footnote chain, this was reported in *The Atlanta Journal* on July 11, 1986 in a profile by Keith Dunnavant titled "Youthful Duluth inventor receives national recognition," which characterized this as the inaugural cohort of the program.[^3] The "inaugural" framing rests on this single newspaper citation and would benefit from independent confirmation against DOE or Brookhaven program records.

This 1986 Brookhaven appointment is foundational to understanding Kirkpatrick's later trajectory. Brookhaven is one of seventeen U.S. Department of Energy national laboratories, operated under a management-and-operating contract for DOE's Office of Science. Kirkpatrick's relationship with the DOE national laboratory system therefore predates not only his AARO directorship but his entire intelligence community career, dating to his late high school years. When in December 2023 he moved to the Oak Ridge National Laboratory — another DOE national laboratory — he was closing a thirty-seven-year arc, not opening a new chapter.

### Higher education

Kirkpatrick attended the University of Georgia (UGA) for both undergraduate and graduate work, receiving:

- **B.S., Physics, University of Georgia, 1991**[^2]
- **Ph.D., Physics, University of Georgia, 1995**[^2]

His doctoral dissertation, supervised by **Dr. William Dennis**, was titled *"Experimental and Theoretical Investigation of Nonequilibrium Acoustic Phonon Dynamics in Praseodymium-Doped Fluorides."*[^2] The substantive subject matter — nonlinear and nonequilibrium phonon dynamics of rare-earth-doped fluoride crystals — would later inform the name of his post-government consulting vehicle, Nonlinear Solutions LLC.

Following his Ph.D., Kirkpatrick conducted postdoctoral research at the **University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign** (1995), investigating laser-induced molecular vibrations of high explosives under an Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR) program.[^1]

### UGA recognition

In 2010, the UGA Department of Physics and Astronomy **established the Sean M. Kirkpatrick Award for Outstanding Graduate Research**, awarded annually to a graduate student.[^9] The award's existence — formally bestowing his name on departmental recognition while he was a serving senior intelligence officer — is documented in the UGA Physics & Astronomy Fall 2010 newsletter, which reported on its first cohort of recipients.[^9] (The newsletter does not specify whether the award was newly established that year or had been in place earlier in the academic year.) He currently holds an appointment as **Adjunct Assistant Professor of Physics** at UGA.[^8]

### The "six new-start organizations" pattern

A critical interpretive frame for Kirkpatrick's career is that, by his own and his subsequent employers' accounts, **he served as either the IC or DoD lead for establishing six "new-start" organizations across his federal service.**[^4][^6][^51] His Elara Nova partner biography and his WestExec senior advisor biography both repeat this six-organization framing nearly verbatim, with AARO listed as the sixth.[^4][^6] The fifth was the USSPACECOM Intelligence Enterprise.[^4][^6]

This distinguishes Kirkpatrick's profile sharply from the conventional reading of him as a generic intelligence physicist. He was a serial new-organization standup specialist — the IC's go-to operator for converting policy authority into operational capacity. AARO was an extension of that pattern, not an outlier. His selection by USD(I&S) Ronald Moultrie *prior to* AARO's establishment is consistent with this credentialing rationale.[^12]

### Timeline notes

The career timeline as presented in his official bios shows an unaccounted transitional period of approximately six to twelve months between his NSC role ending in 2018 and his arrival at USSPACECOM in 2019. The bios do not explain this gap. Such gaps are common in IC-officer career narratives — they may reflect a fellowship, a school assignment, an unannounced rotation, or simply a presentation choice by the bio author. It is worth resolving against primary HR records but is not material to the institutional posture analysis below.

The 2021–2022 **DIA Missile and Space Intelligence Center (MSIC)** Chief Scientist role — Kirkpatrick's immediate pre-AARO position — places him at MSIC in Huntsville, Alabama, where DIA's component conducts technical intelligence analysis on adversary missile and space systems. This role is the technical-intelligence anchor immediately preceding his selection as AARO Director and is consistent with his career-long DIA spine.

## Part III — AARO Tenure (July 20, 2022 – December 1, 2023)

### Establishment context

AARO was established on July 15, 2022 as the renamed successor to the Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronization Group (AOIMSG), which itself had succeeded the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force (UAPTF, 2020–2021).[^14] AARO was created within the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (OUSD(I&S)) under the FY22 NDAA Section 1683 (50 U.S.C. § 3373), with its founding "aided by Senator Kirsten Gillibrand."[^14]

On July 20, 2022, USD(I&S) Ronald S. Moultrie announced the establishment of AARO and named Kirkpatrick — most recently chief scientist at DIA's Missile and Space Intelligence Center — as the office's first director.[^12]

The AARO Executive Council (AAROEXEC) provided oversight and direction. Per Wikipedia citing the relevant primary sources, AAROEXEC was co-chaired by **the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security and the Director for Operations, Joint Staff (J3).**[^14] Moultrie chaired AAROEXEC as USD(I&S).[^14] The Joint Staff J3 across the entirety of Kirkpatrick's AARO tenure (July 2022 – December 2023) was **Lt. Gen. Douglas A. Sims II**, who assumed the position in June 2022.[^11] His immediate predecessor was **Lt. Gen. James J. Mingus**, who held the J3 role from September 2020 to June 2022 and transitioned to Director of the Joint Staff in the same window in which AARO was being established (Mingus moved to DJS in June 2022; AARO was announced publicly July 20, 2022).[^18] Sims continued as J3 through early 2024, and so was the AAROEXEC J3 co-chair across the entire Kirkpatrick directorship.[^11]

### Reporting structure transition — late July 2023

A critical structural change occurred in **late July 2023**, contemporaneous with the July 26, 2023 House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security hearing at which David Grusch testified. According to DefenseScoop (August 30, 2023), citing Pentagon sources directly, **Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks moved to personally oversee AARO and held regular meetings with Kirkpatrick, who was "repositioned to report directly to her."**[^19] The reporting chain shifted from USD(I&S) (Moultrie) to DepSecDef (Hicks).

The same DefenseScoop reporting documents an extraordinary level of OUSD(I&S) micromanagement of AARO's external-facing surface during the prior nine months: **"Between November 2022 and April 2023, that submitted [website] package moved back and forth between the I&S front office and AARO at least every other week. At Moultrie's request, AARO regularly responded to questions, made edits and re-coordinated the memo."**[^19] AARO's website missed its FY23 NDAA-mandated June 2023 launch deadline by approximately ten weeks; it was launched August 30, 2023.[^14][^19]

### Key tenure events

**December 16, 2022 — first joint media roundtable with USD(I&S) Moultrie.**[^15] Established DOE as a partnership channel from establishment forward (mentioned three times). Established the rhetorical boundary that AARO's "holdings" referred to documents and memos, not physical evidence. Moultrie stated publicly that the Department had "not seen anything that would lead us... to believe that any of the objects we have seen are of alien origin."[^14][^15]

**March 2023 — co-authored manuscript with Avi Loeb** on physical constraints of unidentified aerial phenomena, considering the hypothesis that UAPs "could be 'probes' from an extraterrestrial 'parent craft.'"[^16] The unfinished draft was placed online without his clearance, drawing media attention.[^14]

**March 8, 2023 — SAPCO authority memorandum.** Major General David W. Abba, Director of the DoD Special Access Program Central Office (DoD SAPCO), signed a memorandum confirming AARO's formal authority to receive UAP-related information across SAPs, controlled-access programs, and notwithstanding nondisclosure agreements. The memorandum was released to The Black Vault under FOIA case 24-F-0266.[^48]

**April 19, 2023 — sole-witness testimony before the Senate Armed Services Subcommittee on Emerging Threats and Capabilities**, chaired by Senator Kirsten Gillibrand.[^17] Established the institutional NHI-avoidance script that survives Kirkpatrick's departure: "AARO has found no credible evidence thus far of extraterrestrial activity, off-world technology, or objects that defy the known laws of physics."[^17] In testimony Kirkpatrick stated that he reported to USD(I&S) "until they come up with the plan for how they are going to implement legislation."[^17] The same testimony asserted that AARO had "numerous peer reviewed final reports" within its holdings; AARO's separate published reporting in the same window referenced hundreds of identified cases plus an additional ~175 cases set to close. John Greenewald (The Black Vault) FOIA'd both the peer-reviewed reports and the closed-case reports, was told all responsive records were online, and confirmed only five reports were publicly available. Greenewald appealed and won in 2025; the DoD FOIA office is redoing the search.[^52]

**May 31, 2023 — opening remarks at the first public meeting of NASA's UAP Independent Study Team.**[^14] Acknowledged that U.S. military personnel were observing "metallic orbs" in many locations "all over the world," that spherical objects accounted for nearly half of UAP reports, and that some objects were capable of "very interesting apparent maneuvers."[^14]

**June 11–13, 2023 — encrypted Signal text-message exchanges with Christopher Mellon**, former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and known UAP-disclosure advocate. The exchanges, released under FOIA 24-F-0266, document Mellon attempting to mediate between David Grusch and AARO. Kirkpatrick framed his own role to Mellon as "**defending and adjudicating**" AARO, accusing Mellon of "undermining the very organization [Mellon] purported to help establish for this purpose."[^48] The verb "defending" is Kirkpatrick's own contemporaneous self-description of his AARO role.

**July 26, 2023 — David Grusch testified** before the House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security, alleging the existence of a multi-decade U.S. government program to recover and reverse-engineer non-human craft and biological remains. Within days, Hicks took direct AARO oversight (above).[^19]

**July 28, 2023 — Politico interview** in which Kirkpatrick called the Grusch hearing "insulting... to the officers of the Department of Defense and Intelligence Community who chose to join AARO."[^20] Notably, in the same period he used the term "non-human technology" rather than the UAP Disclosure Act's statutory term "non-human intelligence."[^20]

**September 12, 2023 — body-redacted email to "Stone" at DoD OIG.** The email's body content is fully redacted under FOIA exemption (b)(5) deliberative-process privilege; the metadata (recipient name "Stone," recipient agency, date, and that Kirkpatrick was the sender) is visible.[^49] The recipient's first name and full role have not been resolved in public sources. The email's footer asserted deliberative-process privilege as a sender-imposed claim, an unusual practice that The Black Vault has flagged as legally insufficient and is appealing.[^49]

**October 16, 2023 — Nonlinear Solutions LLC registered in North Carolina**, while Kirkpatrick was sitting AARO Director.[^23][^54] Address: a personal residence in Franklin, NC associated with Kirkpatrick. Reported by Ross Coulthart on November 1, 2023.[^23]

**October 2023 — Elara Nova partner photograph uploaded.** The Elara Nova: The Space Consultancy website's photograph of Kirkpatrick is hosted at the URL `elaranova.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/ENweb_Kirkpatrick.jpg` — the `/2023/10/` path component indicates an October 2023 server upload, while Kirkpatrick was still AARO Director.[^6]

**November 7, 2023 — Politico publishes Kirkpatrick's exit interview.**[^21] Quote: "When I took the job, I promised going in that I would do a year, and we would reevaluate. I have decided to stay on until towards the end of this year because there's a couple more things I need to finish."[^21][^25] A longer Politico Magazine follow-up interview ran on November 12, 2023.[^28]

**November 8, 2023 — Hicks issues formal departure statement** "hours before [Tim] Phillips' appointment was announced."[^13][^58] Hicks: Kirkpatrick "served the American people with honesty and integrity, tackling an incredibly difficult mission to explain the unknown."[^13] **Tim Phillips, on assignment from the Office of the Director of National Intelligence, was named Deputy Director and acting director.**[^14][^71]

**November 2023 — OUSD(I&S) submits a 33-page line-by-line rewrite of the proposed UAP Disclosure Act (UAPDA) to congressional negotiators.**[^59][^60] The rewrite substantially weakened independent disclosure mechanisms and was largely adopted as PL 118-31 / FY24 NDAA Subtitle C §§1841–1843.[^60][^61] In a July 17, 2024 interview with Marik von Rennenkampff and a follow-up July 23, 2024 email to journalist Douglas Johnson, Kirkpatrick publicly claimed credit for this institutional defense effort, stating "we convinced Congress last year not to go down that road" and confirming that the defense was institutional rather than politically directed.[^60] This is primary-source evidence — sourced from Kirkpatrick himself — that he understood and described his AARO directorship as having actively defeated independent UAP-disclosure legislation.

**November 24, 2023 — DOE issues a FOIA response denying Kirkpatrick's connection to ORNL** (per Coulthart's December 14, 2023 reporting; the underlying FOIA response document was attached to Coulthart's X tweet but is not currently retrievable through indexed sources).[^24] Whitley Strieber simultaneously reported on this date that Kirkpatrick's ORNL staff listing had "briefly appeared on the ORNL website earlier this month."[^25]

**~December 9, 2023 — Coulthart reports Kirkpatrick is on ORNL's staff list.**[^24] Coulthart: ORNL's response to the reporting was that "they took down the link."[^24]

**December 1, 2023 — official last day at AARO.** Pentagon spokesperson Sue Gough confirmed the date to DefenseScoop.[^22]

**December 14, 2023 — Politico National Security Daily** confirms the ORNL move.[^24]

### AARO institutional posture under Kirkpatrick

Several patterns of AARO's institutional posture under Kirkpatrick are now documented in primary sources:

**The institutional NHI-avoidance script.** Kirkpatrick's April 19, 2023 SASC formulation — "no credible evidence... of extraterrestrial activity, off-world technology, or objects that defy the known laws of physics"[^17] — was preserved nearly verbatim by his successor Jon Kosloski in November 2024 ("no verifiable evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity or technology").[^14] The script survives director transitions, indicating it is institutional rather than personal. The Sancorp Public Affairs / Reporting / Congressional Affairs contract scope (HQ003422C0094, $4.1M ceiling) is the candidate drafting mechanism.[^64] The UAP Disclosure Act's statutory term "non-human intelligence" is systematically replaced with narrower terms in AARO communications.

**The "holdings" rhetorical boundary.** AARO's "holdings" framing — established at the December 16, 2022 roundtable — limited the office's claims about its own evidence base to documents and memos, not physical artifacts.[^15] This boundary preserved deniability about material samples while ostensibly "having no evidence" of the underlying phenomena.

**Caseload trajectory.** April 2023: 650 cases. November 2024 (FY24 Annual Report): 757 reports plus approximately 900 in active archive. February 2026 (per Sue Gough to DefenseScoop): more than 2,000 cases plus approximately 1,000 in active archive. Caseload growth has substantially outpaced public output growth.

**Greenewald's FOIA appeal win on the "peer reviewed reports" question** is documented evidence that AARO's public claims about its analytical output did not match its actual deliverable surface.[^52]

## Part IV — Patents and Intellectual Property

Per his own biographies on multiple firms' websites and his Wikipedia entry, Kirkpatrick "holds 2 open patents."[^2][^4][^6] Both are now identified.

Note on patent term. Under the standard 20-years-from-filing-date term applicable to U.S. utility patents, the H-TPIP patent (US6624915B1, filed February 26, 2002) would have expired around February 2022 — before AARO stood up. The bios' "2 open patents" framing therefore appears to be either stale or to use "open" in a non-standard sense. The Reveal Systems patent family (below) is the only currently-pending or recently-issued IP that meets a strict reading of "open." This is a minor inconsistency in his self-presented bios.

### Patent 1: H-TPIP (US6624915B1, September 23, 2003)

**Title:** "Holographic recording and micro/nanofabrication via ultrafast holographic two-photon induced photopolymerization (H-TPIP)"[^35]

**Filed by:** Science Applications International Corporation (SAIC). **Current assignee:** publicly recorded as Leidos in the patent's published assignment history.[^35][^39] In 2013, SAIC split into two entities: the original parent renamed itself Leidos, and a new spin-off retained the SAIC name. Whether this specific patent's economic interest tracks the Leidos parent or the new SAIC subsidiary depends on the asset allocation in the 2013 split and would require a USPTO assignment-record check to verify definitively.

**Co-inventors:** Sean M. Kirkpatrick, Morley O. Stone, Jeffery W. Baur, Lisa R. Denny, Lalgudi V. Natarajan, Timothy J. Bunning.[^39] Several co-inventors were AFRL personnel; **Morley O. Stone** later became AFRL's Chief Technology Officer.

**Context.** This patent emerges directly from Kirkpatrick's Air Force Research Laboratory ultrafast laser physics work (1997–2003) and was commercialized through SAIC just as he was leaving for the National Reconnaissance Office. SAIC was later split, with the patent assignment passing to Leidos. The patent is on-pattern for Kirkpatrick's documented scientific career.

### Patent 2: The Reveal Systems patent family (May 2019 – February 2023)

**Title:** "System and method for determining influence of channels in a social network"[^36][^37][^38]

**Filed by and assigned to:** Reveal Systems, Inc.[^36] Reveal Systems Inc. is the corporate/IP holder; Reveel Technologies (consumer brand, reveel.it) is the consumer-facing product.[^40]

**Co-inventors:** Erik T. Bogaard, Jason F. Fairchild, Sean M. Kirkpatrick.[^36][^37][^38][^39]

**Patent family chain:**
- **U.S. Provisional Application 62/844,460** — filed **May 7, 2019**, during Kirkpatrick's tenure as Deputy Director of Intelligence at U.S. Space Command[^36]
- **US20200357080A1** — filed May 7, 2020; assigned to Reveal Systems, Inc., June 9, 2020[^36]
- **US20220084137A1** — filed November 30, 2021, published March 17, 2022[^37]
- **US20230206351A1** — filed February 28, 2023, published June 29, 2023[^38]

**Substantive content.** The patent describes a social-media-influence-scoring system that integrates real-purchase data with engagement signals to determine genuine influencer reach, with explicit anti-bot and anti-fake-fan integrity mechanisms.[^36] In the patent's own framing, the technology "addresses the problem that conventional influence scores are often unreliable due to the use of bots and other techniques to artificially increase the number of fans and likes of an influencer's channel."[^36]

**Co-inventor profiles.**

- **Erik T. Bogaard** is a Boston-based serial entrepreneur, currently founder/Solutions Architect at Forge Lab. Self-described 5x founder, sole inventor on six issued U.S. patents in mobile / Internet payments / fintech, including pin-based password-free online checkout (2011). Prior companies include Reveel, Task Force Lab, Boom! Payments, and myBookCrate. He is a normal-looking commercial fintech founder with no apparent intelligence community background.[^42]
- **Jason F. Fairchild** is a Los Angeles–based ad-tech entrepreneur, currently CEO and co-founder of tvScientific, a Connected-TV performance-advertising platform that was recently acquired by Pinterest. He is a normal-looking commercial ad-tech founder with no apparent intelligence community background.[^43]

**Commercial context.** Per Crunchbase, Reveel Technologies has raised a total of approximately $2.7 million across 5 rounds from 8 investors, with the most recent round a Convertible Note on February 1, 2018 led by Cervin Ventures.[^41] Searches across federal contract data systems return no contracts to Reveal Systems Inc. or Reveel Technologies. The entity operates as a normal small commercial seed-stage startup.

**Architectural significance.** The provisional patent application was filed May 7, 2019 — during Kirkpatrick's tenure as USSPACECOM Deputy Director of Intelligence. Continuation filings were prosecuted throughout his subsequent senior IC roles, including his AARO directorship. **Kirkpatrick was a co-inventor on a private commercial consumer-ad-tech IP filing throughout his most senior intelligence community roles.** His specific contribution and consideration are unresolved in public record without access to his Office of Government Ethics Form 278 financial disclosure.

The Reveal Systems patent is the genuine outlier in Kirkpatrick's patent portfolio — H-TPIP fits his AFRL career profile; Reveal Systems does not fit any documented professional skill set and has no apparent connection to his official duties.

A central finding of this profile is that Kirkpatrick's transition out of AARO was not a single discrete event but a **compressed, overlapping multi-channel landing initiated in October 2023, while he was still sitting AARO Director and while AARO was conducting its institutional defense against legislative reform.**

### The narrative-management interpretation

While Kirkpatrick was conducting AARO's institutional defense — including the November 2023 OUSD(I&S) UAPDA rewrite he later took credit for — he was simultaneously orchestrating a multi-channel post-government landing across:

- **Oak Ridge National Laboratory** (negotiated through October–November, surfaced December)
- **Elara Nova: The Space Consultancy** (photo on server October 2023; disclosed publicly later)
- **Nonlinear Solutions LLC** (registered October 16, 2023)
- **WestExec Advisors** (joined formally May 9, 2024 but presumably negotiated earlier)

These channels were not disclosed publicly until various points after his official December 1, 2023 departure. Standard federal ethics rules under the Office of Government Ethics governing Form 278 financial disclosure require executive-branch officials to disclose negotiations regarding post-government employment. The temporal compression of the October 2023 events makes the OGE Form 278 the highest-priority single document for advancing accountability research on this transition.

### ORNL deletion behavior

The ORNL staff profile at `https://www.ornl.gov/staff-profile/sean-m-kirkpatrick` exhibits **at minimum three takedown-related events** across November 2023 – present:

1. **First takedown — November 2023.** The page was visible briefly in early-to-mid November 2023, per Strieber's contemporaneous November 24, 2023 reporting, which noted the listing had "briefly appeared on the ORNL website earlier this month."[^25] By November 24, the page was already gone, and the same date a DOE FOIA response denied the Kirkpatrick-ORNL connection.[^24]
2. **Second takedown — early December 2023.** Per Coulthart's December 14, 2023 reporting, the listing reappeared in late November / early December (after his initial late-November noticing), and was then taken down again following increased media attention: "ORNL's response? — they took down the link."[^24]
3. **Third (apparently permanent) takedown — sometime after January 6, 2024.** Wikipedia editors cited the page on January 6, 2024 as a live URL.[^2] The URL currently returns HTTP 404. Other ORNL staff named Kirkpatrick (Dru C. Kirkpatrick, Collin T. Kirkpatrick) retain live profiles, indicating selective rather than site-wide removal.[^2]

The deletion pattern — page goes up, gets noticed, comes down, eventually permanently — is consistent with active management of public surface around a politically sensitive personnel matter.

### Four documented public-CV omissions

The ORNL position is systematically omitted from at least four of Kirkpatrick's self-presented (or co-presented) public biographies:

1. **Elara Nova partner page** — omits ORNL entirely.[^6]

## Part VII — The Bigger Picture

### The four-corner thesis: DIA / DOE / AARO / ORNL

Kirkpatrick's career sits on four institutional anchors:

1. **DIA** — three documented tenures (2007–2010 joint CIA-DIA; 2012–2016 DIO/S&TI; 2021–2022 MSIC Chief Scientist). The DIA spine is the durable institutional home of his career.
2. **DOE** — the relationship is foundational, dating to the 1986 Brookhaven appointment[^3], reaffirmed operationally in his December 2022 statement that DOE was an AARO partner from establishment forward[^15], and culminating in his post-AARO move to ORNL as COO of the National Security Sciences Directorate[^26]. Critically, DOE-IN classified network access[^26] gives him a continuing, formal information channel that does not require personal-device communications and that is largely opaque to FOIA processes.
3. **AARO** — selected by Moultrie pre-establishment[^12]; the sixth IC/DoD "new-start" organization in his career[^4][^6]. Reporting chain transitioned from USD(I&S) to DepSecDef in late July 2023, in immediate response to the Grusch hearing.[^19]
4. **ORNL** — actual administrative title **COO of NSSD** per UT-Battelle's own FOIA response[^26]; appears under at least three distinct titles across primary-source documents and public settings — one internal administrative title ("Chief Operating Officer for the National Security Sciences Directorate") and two public-facing titles ("Chief Technology Officer for Defense and Intelligence Programs," "Chief Technology Officer, National Security Programs") — plus three additional public framings that omit ORNL entirely (AARO-history-only, "Owner of Nonlinear Solutions LLC," and "CEO, Nonlinear Solutions, LLC"). Reports to NSSD Director Khaleel. Has classified DOE-IN access and OSD file directories.[^26]

### Architectural context

The substantively relevant architecture in which Kirkpatrick operates is anchored at the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (OUSD(I&S)). Per primary-source documents:

- The **Influence and Perception Management Office (IPMO)** was established March 1, 2022 by SecDef and USD(I&S) direction.[^62] Per the FY25 OSD OP-5 budget materials and IPMO charter documents, IPMO's statutory mission includes "deliberate conceal and selective reveal of strategic defense capabilities" and "management of designated compartmented programs."[^62][^63]
- AARO sits in the same OUSD(I&S) cognizant office as IPMO, operating contracts (e.g., HQ003422C0094, Sancorp Consulting LLC, $4.1M ceiling) covering Public Affairs / FOIA / Reporting / Congressional Affairs scope.[^64]

In this framing, Kirkpatrick's role is best understood as a **mid-level, public-facing node** — an institutional defender (his own contemporaneous word) of a larger architecture that would exist regardless of the UAP factual record. The architecture's principal personnel — USD(I&S), DepSecDef, DNI, the AAROEXEC co-chairs — are above him in the chain of authority, and the operational substrate (IPMO, related compartmented programs, and contractor support including Sancorp Consulting LLC) is around him in the contractor ecosystem.

### Kirkpatrick's own framing of his role

The single most important piece of contemporaneous primary-source evidence on Kirkpatrick's self-understanding of his AARO role is the June 2023 Signal text-message exchange with Christopher Mellon released under FOIA 24-F-0266:[^48]

> Kirkpatrick told Mellon he was "**defending and adjudicating**, and [Mellon] was undermining the very organization [Mellon] purported to help establish for this purpose."[^48]

The verb "defending" — used by Kirkpatrick about himself, in real time, on encrypted Signal messaging, six weeks before Hicks took direct AARO oversight — is the most important single semantic data point on the institutional posture of his directorship. He did not understand his role as primarily investigative; he understood it as primarily defensive.
