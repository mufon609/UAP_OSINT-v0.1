## 1. Topic and Investigation Framing

**Topic:** UAP disclosure and the systematic manipulation of public and congressional perception of the UAP topic by U.S. government actors.

**Originating theory (substantiated):** AARO — the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office — operates under institutional incentives beyond its broadly stated goals (investigate UAP, resolve anomalies, support the warfighter, inform the public). The hidden incentives explain its observable institutional behavior.

**Current investigative phase:** Mapping the architecture inside which AARO operates, and from which those incentives derive. The architecture is an OUSD(I&S)-anchored apparatus performing strategic perception management as a defined institutional mission. UAP is one subject domain the architecture operates in. The architecture would exist and function the same way regardless of what UAP turn out to be.

**What this investigation does not claim:** That non-human intelligence exists, that recovered craft are in U.S. government custody, or that any specific UAP report has non-prosaic origin. The architectural finding is independent of those questions. The apparatus has its own institutional incentives — preservation of the OUSD(I&S) program family's operational substrate — that operate regardless of the UAP factual record.

## 4. The November 2023 Rewrite — The Architecture's Hand at the Drafting Level

The OUSD(I&S) red-line of the UAPDA contains ten distinct architectural moves, each defending a specific feature of the apparatus described above. The rewrite is the architecture's incentive structure made visible at the level of statutory text.

### Architectural Move 1 — "Confirmed" gatekeeping insertion

Original: "All Federal Government records related to unidentified anomalous phenomena should be preserved and centralized."

Rewrite: "All Federal Government records related to **confirmed** unidentified anomalous phenomena should be preserved and centralized."

The single word "confirmed" runs throughout. AARO determines confirmation. The Act's scope collapses to whatever AARO chooses to admit into the category.

### Architectural Move 2 — Replace independent Review Board with AARO

Every Section 9005, 9007, 9008, 9009, 9010 reference to "Review Board" is struck through and replaced with "All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office." The Act's central architectural innovation — an independent agency answerable to Congress — is absorbed into the OUSD(I&S)-administered subordinate office it was designed to circumvent.

### Architectural Move 3 — "Shall" to "should"

The Archivist "shall commence establishment" becomes "should commence." Each head of a Government office "shall" becomes "shall should" (literal text — strikethrough on "shall," underline-add of "should"). Mandatory legal obligations converted to hortatory aspirations.

### Architectural Move 4 — "Unclassified" scope insertion

Original: "all Government, Government-provided, or Government-funded records."

Rewrite: "all **unclassified** U.S. Government, Government provided, or Government funded agency records."

Combined with Move 1, the protected scope reduces to "confirmed unclassified UAP records" — a near-empty set.

### Architectural Move 5 — Contractor-records protection inversion (the critical move)

Original Section 9005(a)(2)(C):
> "No unidentified anomalous phenomena record **created by a person or entity outside the Federal Government**... shall be withheld, redacted, postponed for public disclosure, or reclassified."

Rewrite:
> "No relevant unidentified anomalous phenomena records as defined in Section 9004(a)(2) created by a person or entity ~~outside~~ **working on behalf of** the Federal Government..."

The phrase "outside the Federal Government" is struck through; "working on behalf of the Federal Government" is inserted. **This is a 180-degree inversion.** The original UAPDA protected contractor-created records from being withheld. The rewrite makes only "on behalf of" records non-withholdable — meaning records created independently by private parties (witness statements, whistleblower documentation, private-sector R&D records) lose the protection. The architecture is targeting the protection that would have most exposed Layer 4 deliverables.

### Architectural Move 6 — Reverse-FOIA Senior Agency Officials withdrawal authority (new provision)

Brand-new paragraph (3) under Section 9006:
> "WITHDRAWAL OF RECORDS – Senior Agency Officials designated in accordance with E.O. 13526 or any successor Orders may withdraw records in the collection that are determined to be both not related to unidentified anomalous phenomena and properly classified. The Senior Agency Official must notify the congressional defense and intelligence committees 60 days before each record is withdrawn."

Senior Agency Officials — the same EO 13526 classification authorities the original Act was designed to override — get an affirmative power to pull records back out of the Collection after addition.

### Architectural Move 7 — E.O. 13526 standards as withholding ground

Section 9006 grounds prefixed with "In addition to the relevant authorities in Executive Order 13526" and modified so "Original Classification Authority makes a determination" rather than the Review Board. The Act's supremacy clause is preserved on paper but operationally neutered.

### Architectural Move 8 — Privacy Act as additional withholding ground

Original Section 9006(2) limited privacy-based withholding to confidential-source identity. Rewrite replaces with broader Privacy Act language — significantly broader withholding authority.

### Architectural Move 9 — Subpoena/transfer power converted to non-binding requests

Multiple places where the original Review Board "requires" or "shall direct" become AARO "requests" or "should consider."

### Architectural Move 10 — Eminent domain weakened but redirected to AARO

Section 9010 mandatory eminent domain ("The Federal Government shall exercise eminent domain") converted to non-binding ("It is the sense of Congress that the Federal Government should exercise eminent domain appropriately"). But the *targets* are preserved. Per Kirkpatrick's email: "a compromise was proposed to allow for the exercising of eminent domain under AARO's authority to underscore that AARO could compel disclosure of anything, should anything exist." The architecture's revealing move — willing to retain the legal authority *as long as* AARO holds it.

### NARA/ISOO co-authorship — Executive-branch alignment

The "After conferring with NARA/ISOO" notation in the document's introduction tells us the rewrite was negotiated across the federal classification-system bureaucracy. Schumer's December 2023 floor remark — that "declassification of UAP records will be largely up to the same entities that blocked and obfuscated their disclosure for decades" — was well-targeted. The architecture is OUSD(I&S) plus the federal classification ecology.

- Classification authorities retained

- Contractor-records inversion implemented (Move 5)

## 6. The 2024 Reintroduced UAPDA — What Real Architectural Threat Looks Like

On July 11, 2024, Senator Mike Rounds (with Schumer as co-sponsor) reintroduced the UAPDA as Senate Amendment 2610 to the FY25 NDAA. The 2024 text closes every escape route the OUSD(I&S) rewrite opened:

- **Section ll03(8)** — "Government office" includes any entity with possession or control "**including via contract or other agreement**"
- **Section ll03(23)(X)** — UAP record includes records held by "**any private sector person or entity formerly or currently under contract or some other agreement with the Federal Government**"
- **Section ll04(a)(1)(C)** — Collection includes "Government, Government-provided, **or Government-funded records**"
- **Section ll05(a)(2)(C)** — Restores "outside the Federal Government" language; reverses Move 5
- **Section ll07(i)(1)(C)(iii)** — Attorney General subpoena power over private persons
- **Section ll12(a)** — Supremacy clause overrides FOIA, AEA, transclassified foreign nuclear info
- **Section ll15** — **Explicitly repeals the FY24 NDAA Subtitle C** (i.e., explicitly repeals the OUSD(I&S) rewrite)

### Re: Who killed the original UAPDA

Two non-overlapping primary-source claims exist:

**Position A (defense industry / aerospace lobbying):** Asserted by Tucker Carlson, Tim Burchett (D-Tenn.), David Grusch, the Liberation Times reporting, and the political-money analyses (e.g. politicalsaucer Substack on Rogers/Turner Lockheed contributions). Asserts that defense contractors with potential UAP material (Lockheed, BAE, Boeing, General Dynamics, Northrop Grumman, RTX) drove the gutting through campaign contributions to Turner, Rogers, Johnson, McConnell.

**Position B (Pentagon/AARO defense):** Asserted by Douglas Dean Johnson, supported by Sean Kirkpatrick's on-record statements ("We convinced Congress last year not to go down that road"), and by the November 2023 OUSD(I&S) rewrite document. Asserts that the gutting originated in the Pentagon/AARO institutional position and was largely heeded by Republican lawmakers — not driven by aerospace industry lobbying.

**Status: ❌ Contradiction (with primary-source weight on Position B).** Both positions assert real causal mechanisms, but Position B has direct primary-source documentation (the rewrite itself, Kirkpatrick's email, his on-record statements). Position A documents a contributing influence ecosystem but does not have an equivalent smoking-gun document. The two are not mutually exclusive — the architecture and aerospace industry could both have wanted the gutting — but Position B is the documented primary-source mechanism.
