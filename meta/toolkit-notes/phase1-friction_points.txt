Phase I friction — concrete points                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                   
1. Prose-drift inflection sensitivity. The tokenizer is morphology-strict — holds failed but hold passed; documented failed but document/documents       
passed; informal failed but informally passed; coordinated failed but coordination/coordinating passed. Required multiple check-vocab.py round-trips per 
draft. Working with the discipline, not against it — but real overhead.                                                                                  
2. YAML single-quoted scalar trailing-apostrophe trap. Embedding source-quoted phrases (e.g., 'Importance: High', 'The Pentagon Disinformation … 
Mythology') inside single-quoted YAML scalars escapes to 'Importance: High' in the loaded value, and the tokenizer keeps the trailing apostrophe as part 
of the last word — high', mythology', both registered as unmatched tokens. The fix was to restructure prose to avoid embedded source-quoted phrases
(those should live in quotes[], not in synthesis prose). Worth knowing for future contributors.                                                          
3. Hyphenation = one token. FOIA-released tokenized as one absent token; FOIA case 24-F-0894 release worked. Same for pa-odni → OSD(PA) and ODNI. Easy to
fix once you see it; not obvious upfront.                                                                                                               
4. Location form for the FOIA .txt sibling. Conventions.md says paginated PDFs use p. N, ¶M, but the FOIA .txt sibling is contributor-VLM-transcribed and
doesn't carry page anchors. The AARO artifact uses descriptive prose locations (Sue Gough email to ODNI counterpart, March 5, 2024 12:51 PM) for the    
same source. I followed that established practice. Real divergence from the canonical form, but matches what AARO + ONI + UAPTF do — so it's de facto
convention for FOIA email releases.                                                                                                                      
5. 2003 USAWC paper question — genuine ambiguity, resolved deliberately. The manifest note conflates Susan I. Gough (USAWC paper) with Susan L (Sue) 
Gough (FOIA emails). Middle initial differs; no bridging primary source. Per the source-priority and investigate-before-queueing memories I omitted it   
from primary_sources and registered it as rumors.r1 instead of importing the contributor inference silently.
6. One Edit-tool failure on the long credibility_notes rewrite. Had to break it into smaller targeted edits. Mechanical, no real friction.
