## Summary

Ryan Graves's ([`/people/ryan-graves`]) sworn oral testimony at the July 26, 2023 House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security, the Border, and Foreign Affairs hearing on unidentified anomalous phenomena ([`/events/2023-07-26-house-uap-hearing`]). Graves is one of three witnesses sworn at the hearing; David Fravor ([`/people/david-fravor`]) and David Grusch ([`/people/david-grusch`]) also testified. Graves's written testimony ([`/documents/written-testimony-graves-2023`]) was also submitted for the record.

Graves's opening statement — former F–18 pilot with a decade of service in the U.S. Navy ([`/organizations/us-navy`]) including Operation Enduring Freedom; here to voice the concerns of more than 30 commercial aircrew and military veterans who have confided their similar encounters; UAP are in our airspace but grossly under reported, these sightings are not rare or isolated, they are routine; the stigma attached to UAP is real and powerful, silences commercial pilots who fear professional repercussions, with recent government claims questioning the credibility of eyewitness testimony; excessive classification practices with all UAP videos classified as secret or above since 2021; in 2014 an F–18 Foxtrot pilot in the Navy Fighter Attack Squadron 11 ([`/organizations/vfa-11`]) the Red Rippers stationed at NAS Oceana ([`/organizations/nas-oceana`]) in Virginia Beach with radar upgrades detecting unknown objects; began to correlate the radar tracks with multiple onboard sensors including infrared systems, eventually through visual ID; Warning Area W–72 training mission 10 miles off the coast of Virginia Beach where two F–18 Super Hornets were split by a UAP — a dark gray or a black cube inside of a clear sphere that came within 50 feet of the lead aircraft and was estimated to be five to 15 feet in diameter; the mission commander terminated the flight and returned to base; founded Americans for Safe Aerospace ([`/organizations/americans-for-safe-aerospace`]) as a haven for UAP witnesses previously unspoken due to the absence of a safe intake process — more than thirty witnesses have come forward and almost 5,000 Americans have joined at safeaerospace.org; the majority of witnesses commercial pilots at major airlines often veterans with decades of flying experience reporting UAP at altitudes above them at 40,000 feet or in low Earth orbit or in the gray zone below the common line, making unexplainable maneuvers like right hand turns and retrograde orbits or J hooks, with reoccurring sightings north of Hawaii and in the North Atlantic; and the closing that if UAP are foreign drones it is an urgent national security problem, if it is something else it is an issue for science, and in either case unidentified objects are a concern for flight safety.

Graves's Q&A exchange under oath — national security concerns on advanced sensors in tactical jets, but no reason the objects themselves would be classified; identifying friend from foe is important and continuing to ignore unidentified targets due to stigma is an opening our adversaries can take advantage of; recommendation for a centralized location with a two-way loop so operators have feedback on reporting; commercial pilots reaching out through Americans for Safe Aerospace because they do not feel there is another way to report this safety issue; objects performing maneuvers unexplainable due to our current understanding of our technology and our capabilities as a country; kinematics — these objects were staying completely stationary in category four hurricane winds, then would accelerate to 1.1 to 1.2 Mach in very quick behaviors he does not have an explanation for; objects detected essentially where all Navy operations are being conducted across the world from All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office ([`/organizations/aaro`]) reporting; recognized voices and video from the 2017 New York Times article ([`/documents/2017-nyt-tic-tac`]) because he had seen it when it was taken and debriefed; dark gray or black cube inside of a clear sphere with the apex or tips of the cube touching the inside of that sphere as primarily reported shape over almost 8 years and still occurring; 2003 Vandenberg Air Force Base ([`/locations/vandenberg-afb`]) event — Boeing ([`/organizations/boeing`]) contractors observed a very large 100-yard sighted red square approach from the ocean and hover at low altitude over one of the launch facilities for about 45 seconds with a similar evening event post sunset including aggressive behaviors approaching security guards; Gimbal video ([`/media/gimbal-declassified`]) — some uncertainty or instability with the object seemed to rock a bit; privy to conversations with commercial aviators who have received cease and desist orders; objects observed spherical up to Mach 2 which is a very nonaerodynamic shape; a scientific problem and an engineering problem as much as a national security problem with collaboration with a professional aerospace organization ([`/organizations/aiaa`]) to stand up a UAP integration committee; AARO data should prioritize kinematics and the specifics of how vehicles or objects are moving plus a more zoomed out approach on origin and destination plus a better contextual understanding; the United States Navy Range Fouler Report gathers information from pilots with a service-wide reporting mechanism still pending; part of what keeps information from the American people has been not encouraging reporting; Gimbal incident — AT FLIR system shows a single object that rotates while pilots refer to a fleet of objects that is not visible on the FLIR system but witnessed during the debrief as part of the radar data on the situational awareness page; approximately 5 percent reporting estimate somewhere near that number; sworn affirmative that the Navy took the danger to his aircraft seriously after it was reported; most vivid near mid-air at the entrance to his working area with a pilot flying within about 50 feet of a completely stationary object who landed in the ready room with his mouth open and said he almost hit one of those darn things — dark gray or black cube inside of a clear sphere with no wings, no IR energy coming off of the vehicle, and nothing tethering it to the ground; came forward because his colleagues did not have a way to mitigate the safety threat and he was trained as an aviation safety officer by the Navy; and no public options for the everyman to report with a recommendation for technology solutions that can be distributed so that objective data can be gathered.
