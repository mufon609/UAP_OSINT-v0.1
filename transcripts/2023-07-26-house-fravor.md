## Summary

David Fravor's ([`/people/david-fravor`]) sworn oral testimony at the July 26, 2023 House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security, the Border, and Foreign Affairs hearing on unidentified anomalous phenomena ([`/events/2023-07-26-house-uap-hearing`]). Fravor is one of three witnesses sworn at the hearing; Ryan Graves ([`/people/ryan-graves`]) and David Grusch ([`/people/david-grusch`]) also testified. Fravor's written testimony ([`/documents/written-testimony-fravor-2023`]) was also submitted for the record.

Fravor's opening statement — his service as retired Commander in the United States Navy ([`/organizations/us-navy`]); 2004 commanding officer of Strike Fighter Squadron 41 ([`/organizations/vfa-41`]), tasked to Carrier Airwing 11 ([`/organizations/carrier-airwing-eleven`]) on board the USS Nimitz ([`/organizations/uss-nimitz`]); 2 V 2 air to air training with USS Princeton ([`/organizations/uss-princeton`]) as control; the 2004 Tic Tac encounter ([`/events/2004-nimitz-encounter`]); the 2009 Jay Stratton ([`/people/jay-stratton`]) contact; the ATIP program in the Pentagon led by Lue Elizondo ([`/people/luis-elizondo`]); the Alex Dietrich ([`/people/alex-dietrich`]) contact; the Elizondo contact; the formation of To the Stars Academy ([`/organizations/ttsa`]) with Tom DeLonge ([`/people/tom-delonge`]), Chris Mellon ([`/people/christopher-mellon`]), Steve Justice ([`/people/steve-justice`]) and others; the 2017 New York Times articles by Leslie Keane ([`/people/leslie-kean`]), Ralph Blumenthal ([`/people/ralph-blumenthal`]) and Helene Cooper ([`/people/helene-cooper`]); and the closing concern for oversight from elected officials on craft believed not from this world.

### Tic Tac visual description — 4 observers, white, north-south axis, ping-pong-ball movement, no rotors/wash/control surfaces; WSO/clockwise/high-cover tactical setup

> All four of us, because we were an F–18F so we had pilots and WSO in the backseat, looked down and saw a white Tic Tac object with a longitudinal axis pointing north south and moving very abruptly over the water like a ping pong ball. There were no rotors, no rotor wash, or any sign of visible control surfaces like wings. As we started clockwise toward the object my WSO and I decided to go down and take a closer look with the other aircraft staying in high cover to observe both us and the Tic Tac.
