## Summary

WEAPONIZED part one of two with Dr. James Lacatski ([`/people/james-lacatski`]) with George ([`/people/george-knapp`]) and Jeremy ([`/people/jeremy-corbell`]) — almost two years after WEAPONIZED ([`/transcripts/weaponized-038-lacatski-kelleher-2023`]).

Lacatski said "I was the creator and DIA ([`/organizations/dia`]) program manager of AASAP ([`/organizations/aawsap`]), the Advanced Aerospace Weapons System Applications Program, and of the follow-on Kona Blue program, KBP. Both classified programs, regardless of what the internet has said in the past. Both classified." Lacatski says Jeremy asked him to testify before Congress and Lacatski's answer is in the four books — Skinwalkers at the Pentagon, Inside the US Government Covert UFO Program: Initial Revelations, the new New Insights, and the approved future book Future Visions. Lacatski says the books are "everything that I have been approved to say condensed into what will be 1,200 pages" and "all I could ever say to the Congress, and they can read it and know a hundred times more than they're ever going to get from hearings." Lacatski says the books are "approved for release by the Department of Defense or War."

Lacatski says "I don't suspect, wonder, or harbor any suspicion that false statements have been made to Congress, to the Pentagon, to you, the press, and on the internet. I know they have been. I know it. Deliberately false, not just incorrect, documents have been created about AASAP and Kona Blue, and are in currently in government files." Lacatski says he reported some of the false information to the DOD/DOW Inspector General's Office and requested IG take no action against the individuals because the falsehoods "to my mind have been ineffective." Lacatski says the 2017 New York Times story was "totally inaccurate" and that the authors "knew my name and position at AATIP, yet never attempted to contact me"; the Pentagon ([`/organizations/dod`]) "states that AATIP really AATIP was not a UFO program. They've changed their tune, but the Pentagon has never asked me about anything. They circumvented me and went to my colleagues, but not me."

Lacatski says he has been requested to meet with AARO ([`/organizations/aaro`]) twice and has refused. He said an AARO email said: "Holding a meeting with you in a secure space is very easy. Under our authorities, you do not need a current clearance. The law also grants us additional security authorities. Under a special program we can hold discussions at any level including human." Lacatski says HUMINT is controlled by the gathering organization and cannot be discussed across the lines without a ton of paperwork. Lacatski says AARO is reviewing his books — "AARO is reviewing books? Wow." Lacatski reports a former deputy director of AARO disparaging the UAP and paranormal connection — said "they were looking for werewolves" and that "Kona Blue was established as the home for all these paranormal UFO crap." Lacatski says "Something serious is going on. In my opinion, you all are in the middle of a what I can only describe as a major and repetitive counterintelligence operation by unknown multiple operators or organizations and for unknown reasons. Big question. What is so important about AATIP or Kona Blue?" Lacatski says "Arrow's a disinformation campaign you're coming to realize by their public statements and how they're talking about a program that you have knowledge of that they're completely lying to the American public" — he then says it is more "steering the direction of the conversation" than completely lying.

On Kona Blue: Lacatski says "Kona Blue is much more than... Kona Blue, I can answer that directly, but I can't go into details. Kona Blue was a compartment. It was also a program." Lacatski says he should not be using the past tense for Kona Blue. "A program continues. It can be unfunded, it can be funded, it can be temporarily funded. Money can go in and out. So, a UFO study program could continue under that name." Lacatski says Kelleher suggested "private money. Private money going in from multiple sources" — in the rear portions of Skinwalkers at the Pentagon. Lacatski says "The program was not killed. You can't kill Kona Blue." Lacatski says he met with Dr. Tar O'Toole ([`/people/tara-otoole`]) and that he wants to write another book on Kona Blue from his perspective.

On the UFO craft: Jeremy said "we have a UFO in government possession, that we have an exploitation effort on on vehicle, that we've had that we've been successful in at least penetrating the hull." When asked whether the United States "has a reverse engineering program on that UFO if you breached the hull," Lacatski says "I can't answer that. It goes beyond what I've been approved to say." Lacatski says "if you're looking at such a craft, what are you primarily interested in? The craft? The fact that it's empty? Or what in the world is the power supply and the motive force?" Lacatski says "a fair amount of money went into the medical aspect of UFOs in and AATIP, much more so than was described." Lacatski says "where are the pilots? Who were they? Where did they go if there were pilots?" Asked whether the craft was "landed, crashed, or gifted to us," Lacatski says "You're relentless, aren't you?" and does not answer. The 10-month BAASS ([`/organizations/baass`]) report was "stolen" — never numbered, may be two complete bound copies still out there.
