## Summary

Sean Kirkpatrick's ([`/people/sean-kirkpatrick`]) open session testimony at the April 19, 2023 Senate Armed Services Subcommittee on Emerging Threats and Capabilities hearing on the mission, activities, oversight, and budget of the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office ([`/events/2023-04-19-sasc-aaro-hearing`]). Kirkpatrick was the witness; today's earlier closed session of the same hearing is not in the open transcript. The Subcommittee met, pursuant to notice, at 11:08 a.m. in Room SR-232A, Russell Senate Office Building. Senator Kirsten E. Gillibrand ([`/people/kirsten-gillibrand`]), chairwoman of the subcommittee, presiding; Subcommittee Members Present: Senators Gillibrand, Rosen ([`/people/jacky-rosen`]), and Ernst ([`/people/joni-ernst`]). The hearing was adjourned at 12:08 p.m. Stenographic transcript by Alderson Court Reporting. Kirkpatrick's Statement for the Record ([`/documents/written-testimony-kirkpatrick-2023`]) was submitted for the record.

Kirkpatrick's opening statement described AARO's establishment, structure (a team of more than three dozen experts organized around four functional areas: operations, scientific research, integrated analysis, and strategic communications), legislative direction to carefully review and research the U.S. government's UAP-related historical record, the priority on UAP reports by DOD and IC personnel in or near areas of national security importance, and the statement clearly for the record that AARO has found no credible evidence thus far of extraterrestrial activity, off world technology, or objects that defy the known laws of physics. He says AARO's mission is to turn UAP into SEP, somebody else's problem, and says AARO is committed to the scientific method, peer review, and being methodical and scrupulous. A slide presentation goes through trend analysis (over half — about 52 percent — of reported UAP are round or spheres) and two declassified MQ-9 case examples (a Middle East apparent spherical object via EO sensors, and a South Asia case where an apparent propulsion trail turns out to be a sensor artifact and the object turns out to be a commuter aircraft).

Q&A has a first round and a second round. Senator Gillibrand asked about AARO's cases (a total of 650 cases as of that week, up from about 366 in January), the five-step analytic process AARO has set up (an intelligence community team and an S&T team made up as a red team, blue team, or competitive analysis, with a senior technical advisory group peer review), AARO's reporting chain (Kirkpatrick says he is currently reporting to USDINS until DOD and DNI come up with the plan to implement the fiscal year 2023 National Defense and Intelligence Authorization Act direct reporting chain to the Deputy Secretary of Defense), the publicly discoverable safe disclosure website (submitted for approval before Christmas, still under review), and AARO's role in DOD's response to the recent UAP incidents over North America (initial advice only). Senator Ernst asked about foreign nexus assessment ([`/organizations/odni`] annual threat assessment on China and Russia), and Kirkpatrick says he has concerning indicators that some cases are related to foreign capabilities and that the adversary is not waiting. Senator Rosen asked about aviation safety and Joint Staff coordination, and Kirkpatrick described coming back from sitting down at NORTHCOM with General VanHerck ([`/people/glen-vanherck`]) and the J staff. Gillibrand says she will help write legislative language for additional collection tasking and counter-intelligence authorities Kirkpatrick says the office needs.

At the close of the open session, two audience members came forward and presented evidence to Kirkpatrick — a Marine who drove 600 miles to come and presented a book to David Spergel ([`/people/david-spergel`]) of the NASA ([`/organizations/nasa`]) UAP independent study, and an audience member from Albuquerque, New Mexico who described glowing dots flying over Sandia Labs and Kirkland Air Force Base. Senator Gillibrand says her office can be a clearinghouse for that information and the hearing was adjourned at 12:08 p.m.
