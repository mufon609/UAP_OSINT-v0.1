# Lockheed Martin — Non-Denial Pattern

## Pattern Statement

Three discrete Liberation Times-documented moments (November 2023, multiple occasions across 2024 and early 2025, March 3 2025) of Lockheed Martin refusing to issue categorical denials of allegations that it possesses or has handled UAP / non-human material.

---

## Description

Christopher Sharp ([`/people/christopher-sharp`]) at Liberation Times ([`/organizations/liberation-times`]) ([`/documents/liberationtimes-burlison-grusch-recruitment-20250327`]) reported: "Lockheed Martin ([`/organizations/lockheed-martin`]) has refused on multiple occasions to comment on allegations that its executives, including former Vice President of Space Systems, James Ryder ([`/people/james-ryder`]), were involved in the attempted transfer of materials." In November 2023 Liberation Times asked Lockheed Martin whether it could deny allegations that it possesses materials of unknown or non-human origin; a company spokesperson stated: "Questions about UAPs are best addressed by the U.S. government." On 3 March 2025 Liberation Times contacted Lockheed Martin's media relations office with the following inquiries: the company's involvement in the proposed material divestment plan, whether Lockheed Martin currently possesses or has previously handled advanced non-human materials linked to historical crash retrieval operations, and whether the company holds any records of Ryder's alleged role. "Despite repeated requests for comment, Lockheed Martin has refused to respond." Sharp had earlier reported on 8 November 2024 ([`/documents/liberationtimes-witnesses-announced-landmark-ufo-hearing-20241108`]) that the alleged non-human materials "Lockheed Martin may still possess to this day"; no Lockheed Martin denial followed.

## What the Record Establishes

- Lockheed Martin declined to issue a denial when Liberation Times asked directly in November 2023 whether it could deny allegations of possessing materials of unknown or non-human origin (per q1).
- Lockheed Martin "refused on multiple occasions to comment on allegations" of executive involvement in the proposed material transfer (per q2 — Sharp's aggregate attestation in 2025-03-27).
- Lockheed Martin did not respond to direct media-relations-office inquiries from Liberation Times on March 3 2025 covering company involvement in the proposed transfer, current or prior handling of non-human material, and records of Ryder's alleged role (per q3 and q4).
- Lockheed Martin issued no public denial after Liberation Times published the Gaffney-blocked-transfer allegation in the 2024-11-08 article that named Ryder and stated the materials "Lockheed Martin may still possess to this day" (per q5; absence of subsequent denial is contextual).
- The non-denial pattern is consistent across the sixteen-month window from November 2023 to March 2025 across multiple journalistic touchpoints with Sharp's reporting at Liberation Times.

---

## What the Record Doesn't Establish

- Whether Lockheed Martin currently possesses materials of unknown or non-human origin — refusing to comment is consistent with continued custody, with corporate-policy non-engagement on UAP allegations, or with no factual underlying material to comment on.
- The reason for Lockheed Martin's non-denial posture — legal exposure, classification constraints, corporate communications policy, factual ambiguity, and other explanations are all consistent with the pattern.
- Independent corroboration of the non-denial pattern outside Sharp's Liberation Times reporting — the pattern is documented through one outlet's sustained outreach to the company.
- Whether Lockheed Martin would issue a categorical denial under different conditions — sworn testimony, formal Securities and Exchange Commission filing, or direct congressional inquiry have not (per archived primary sources) been the form of any documented inquiry to Lockheed Martin to date on this allegation cluster.
