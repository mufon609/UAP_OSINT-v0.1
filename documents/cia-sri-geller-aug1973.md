## Description

Records of picture drawing experiments performed with Uri Geller ([`/people/uri-geller`]) at SRI ([`/organizations/stanford-research-institute`]) over the eight days August 4 through August 11, 1973. Text pages are dated August 13, 1973 with R. Targ ([`/people/russell-targ`]). Approved For Release 2003/03/28.

The OBJECTIVE on page 1: 'to verify Geller's apparent paranormal perception under carefully controlled conditions and to head toward an understanding of the physical and psychological variables underlying his apparent ability'. In each of the eight days the experimenters separated Geller from the target material either by an electrically isolated shielded room or by the isolation provided by having the targets drawn on the East Coast. Six days of work were performed with the double-walled shielded room used for EEG research in the Life Sciences Division of SRI, locked by an inner and outer door each secured with a refrigerator-type locking mechanism. On Thursday August 9 the experiments moved to a new shielded room in the Engineering building, about 150 feet down the hall and around a corner from the computer room, to make use of the computer facilities available there.

Page 1 records that 'as a result of Geller's success in this experimental period, we consider that he has demonstrated his paranormal perceptual ability in a convincing and unambiguous manner'. The same page also records that six days of work were performed with the Life Sciences shielded room and that good results were obtained on the four days when there was no openly skeptical observer. Target and Geller response across the eight days are shown in the text and attached collection of drawings: 'fuse' / firecracker (a cylinder with noise coming out of it, drum); 'bunch' / bunch of grapes (drops of water coming out of the picture, 24 grapes in both target and response); a man which evolved through the drawing process into a devil with a trident (Sunday August 5 with Puthoff ([`/people/hal-puthoff`]) inside the room with Geller and Targ in the experimenter's office about a half mile away with the drawing; Garden of Eden picture; Geller drew the trident but not the man); the solar system without orbit lines (Geller's response: 'space'); a pure clairvoyance task with a rabbit (passed); a tree and an envelope with EEG apparatus (passed); a camel (horse); the Golden Gate Bridge (curved lines with squares, passed); a flying sea gull (flying swan over a hill); a kite drawn on the face of the TV screen by the computer's graphics program (square with the diagonals drawn in, then triangular airplanes); a church drawn and stored in the memory of the computer (passed); an arrow through a heart drawn on the face of the TV tube with the intensity turned off (arrow inside a suitcase); a long distance experiment with an East Coast scientist (two peaked mountains with a sun in the upper right / two arches side by side with a circle in the upper right); and a Saturday August 11 long distance experiment with another East Coast scientist who chose a cross-sectional view of the brain (Geller writing 'medical', 'organic', 'living', 'aviation', 'architecture', and a drawing that did somewhat resemble an anatomical cross-section).

### Shielded-room facility description — double-walled EEG-research room, Life Sciences Division SRI; refrigerator-type locking mechanism

> Saturday, August 4. Two drawing experiments were conducted this day. In both of these, Geller was closeted in an opaque, acoustically and electrically shielded room. This room is the double-walled shielded room used for EEG research in the Life Sciences Division of SRI. It is locked by means of an inner and outer door, each of which is secured with a refrigerator-type locking mechanism.

### Protocol-controls attestation — one-way intercom, no sensory leakage found in detailed examination

> In this work the target picture is never discussed by the experimenters after the picture is drawn or brought near the shielded room. The intercom operates only from the inside of the room to the outside, except when the push-to-talk switch is depressed on the outside of the room. In our detailed examination of the shielded room and the protocol used in these experiments no sensory leakage has been found, nor has any defect in the protocol been brought to our attention.

### Per-day narrative — clairvoyance task with target drawn by outside-of-group scientist; Geller passed on the rabbit target

> Monday, August 6. The experiment to be done this day was a pure clairvoyance task. A picture was drawn by a scientist outside of the usual experimental group. The picture was locked in the shielded room before Geller's arrival at SRI. Geller was then led by the experimenters to the shielded room, and asked to draw the picture inside the room. He drew a number of pictures, all of which he rejected as not being applicable. He said that he got no clear impression and passed.

### Per-day narrative — EEG apparatus session; tree and envelope targets; Geller passed on both

> Tuesday, August 7. This day two target pictures were attempted with Geller in the shielded room. He was connected to an EEG apparatus to allow measurement of his brain waves at the time that he was attempting to perceive a hidden picture. The two target pictures were a tree and an envelope. He experienced difficulty, did not make a drawing that corresponded to either drawing and passed.
