## Description

Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick ([`/people/sean-kirkpatrick`]) Statement for the Record, Senate Armed Services Committee on Emerging Threats and Capabilities ([`/organizations/sasc`]), April 19, 2023 ([`/events/2023-04-19-sasc-aaro-hearing`]) ([`/transcripts/2023-04-19-sasc-kirkpatrick`]).

Kirkpatrick: "Thank you, Chairwoman Gillibrand ([`/people/kirsten-gillibrand`]), Ranking Member Ernst ([`/people/joni-ernst`]), and distinguished members of the Subcommittee and Congress. It is a privilege to be here today to testify on the Department of Defense ([`/organizations/dod`])'s efforts to address Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena."

AARO ([`/organizations/aaro`]) establishment in July of last year. The AARO team of more than three dozen experts is organized around four functional areas: operations, scientific research, integrated analysis, and strategic communications. In the nine months since AARO's establishment, important steps to improve UAP data collection, standardize the Department's UAP internal reporting requirements, and implement a framework for rigorous scientific and intelligence analysis, resolving cases in a systematic and prioritized manner. Consistent with legislative direction, AARO is also carefully reviewing and researching the U.S. Government's UAP-related historical record.

AARO has found no credible evidence thus far of extraterrestrial activity, off-world technology, or objects that defy the known laws of physics. In the event sufficient scientific data were ever obtained that a UAP encountered can only be explained by extraterrestrial origin, AARO is committed to working with interagency partners at NASA ([`/organizations/nasa`]) to appropriately inform the U.S. Government's leadership of its findings. Only a very small percentage of UAP reports display signatures that could reasonably be described as "anomalous" — the majority demonstrate mundane characteristics of balloons, unmanned aerial systems, clutter, natural phenomena, or other readily explainable sources.

AARO is partnering with the Services, Intelligence Community, DOE ([`/organizations/doe`]) and across the U.S. government to tap into the resources of the interagency. AARO's work will take time if committed to doing it right — adhering to the scientific method and the highest standards of research integrity, being methodical and scrupulous, withholding judgment in favor of evidence, following the data where it leads.
