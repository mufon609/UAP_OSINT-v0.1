## Description

The SAFIRE Project ([`/organizations/safire-project`]) is a plasma physics laboratory project proposed by Montgomery Childs ([`/people/montgomery-childs`]) to test the Electric Sun Model. The project's own SAFIRE PROJECT Genesis report (copyright 2017 Aurtas International, patents pending): "The Stellar Atmospheric Function in Regulation Experiment (SAFIRE) was initiated. Its objective was to test the Electric Sun model." The project's own genesis page: "In 2012 at a conference exploring the electrical nature of deep space Montgomery Childs proposed a way the Electric Sun Model could be tested." David Talbott ([`/people/david-talbott`]) "recognized in Monty Childs' SAFIRE concept a real and empircal method for testing the Electric Sun model, and so invited Monty to propose his idea to the Electric Universe Group at the 2012 Electric Universe Conference"; Scott Mainwaring ([`/people/scott-mainwaring`]) "recognized in Montgomery Child's proposal a pragmatic empirical method to test the Electric Universe hypothesis"; Scott "introduced Monty's proposal to his father, Bruce Mainwaring ([`/people/bruce-mainwaring`]). Father and son decided to support the project through the Mainwaring Archive Foundation." Aurtas International, Inc. ([`/organizations/aurtas-international`]) is Aureon Energy's predecessor company; the Aureon Energy team page: Childs "is the chairman and founder of Aureon Energy and its predecessor company, Aurtas International, Inc, where he is the creator, chief scientist, and program leader of the SAFIRE Project". The Aurtas International location is Mississauga according to Jan Onderco ([`/people/jan-onderco`])'s Aureon Energy biography. The project is unfolding in three major phases: a fully working proof-of-concept miniature (bench-top) lab (SAFIRE I in the lab 2013 according to the Aureon Energy history page), a full-size SAFIRE laboratory at the heart of which was the SAFIRE Plasma Engine (SAFIRE II in the lab 2019), and a third phase focused on the molecular and atomic levels of reactions observed in the apparatus.

The SAFIRE Project corporate team page: "This is the team in the years of the International Science Foundation ([`/organizations/international-science-foundation`]) mandate to test the Electric Sun model. As SAFIRE has become a commercial enterprise under the auspices of AUREON ENERGY, Ltd. the team has been expanding." The team page lists the Mainwaring Archive Foundation and the International Science Foundation as foundation partners; the SAFIRE PROJECT Genesis report (p. 9): "Funding for an initial test was promised by the Mainwaring Archive Foundation, to be administered by the International Science Foundation." Dr. William A. Gardner ([`/people/william-gardner`])'s biography on his cyclostationarity research site, "1 The International Science Foundation funded the SAFIRE project for several years and then closed down at the time of its Founder/COB, Dr. James T. Ryder ([`/people/james-ryder`])'s, death in May 2018." The SAFIRE Project team page lists Ryder under science review team with Harold Puthoff PhD ([`/people/hal-puthoff`]) (Director, Institute for Advanced Studies at Austin ([`/organizations/institute-for-advanced-studies-at-austin`])), Eric W. Davis Ph.D. ([`/people/eric-davis`]) (Chief Science Officer, Institute for Advanced Studies at Austin), William A. Gardner (Professor Emeritus, University of California), and Hathaway Consulting Services ([`/organizations/hathaway-consulting-services`]). The project's own commercialization page: "THE SAFIRE PROJECT succesfully completed its original mandate to test the Electric Sun. The decision was then made to take all the discoveries of the previous six years and employ them in producing a commercial plasma reactor that does not generate radioactive waste." The same page: "The new SAFIRE PROJECT, now a commercial enterprise, will be headed by Aureon Energy Ltd." ([`/organizations/aureon-energy`]) The Aureon Energy history page: "Aureon Energy Ltd.  Midland, Ontario,   Canada" with Childs as "Founder and Chairman of Aureon Energy". E-Cat World ([`/organizations/e-cat-world`]) on March 8, 2020: "A new website for Aureon Energy, a company to commercialize the SAFIRE project, has been published at https://aureon.ca". The same article: "Hal Puthoff, a familiar name to many, is a member of their Science Advisory Board."

The Electric Sun Model SAFIRE was built to test is part of the Electric Universe hypothesis. The project's own historical page: "It is now acknowledged that magnetic fields pervade the Universe, and it was Alfvén who noted that these fields cannot exist without electric currents. According to the evidence from satellites and space-based telescopes, 99.9% of the visible Universe appears to be made up of plasma." The project's own Phase Three page lists production capabilities including "Production of low energy quiescent plasmas", "Production of multi spherical plasma double layer shells", and "Potential of Low-Energy Nuclear Reactions (LENR)"; the same page: "SAFIRE is generating variations in electron density comparable to the photoshpere, heliosphere and nuclear bombs" and "The core of SAFIRE is cooler than its surrounding atmoshere". A "90 minute SAFIRE PROJECT presentation" was given "at the 2017 Electric Universe FUTURE SCIENCE Conference in Phoenix Arizona" by Childs and Dr. Michael Clarage ([`/people/michael-clarage`]).
