## Background

Ronald S. "Ron" Moultrie is an American intelligence official who served as the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security (USD/I&S) from June 1, 2021 to February 29, 2024. Confirmed by the Senate on May 29, 2021, Moultrie was the principal advisor to the Secretary of Defense on intelligence, counterintelligence, and security matters. He exercised authority, direction, and control on behalf of the Secretary of Defense over all intelligence and security organizations within the Department of Defense ([`/organizations/dod`]), including the National Security Agency, the Defense Intelligence Agency ([`/organizations/dia`]), the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency ([`/organizations/nga`]), the National Reconnaissance Office ([`/organizations/nro`]), the Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency ([`/organizations/dcsa`]), and the intelligence components of the combatant commands and military services. He was also dual-hatted as the Director of Defense Intelligence in the Office of the Director of National Intelligence and reported to the DNI in this capacity. He was preceded by Joseph D. Kernan and succeeded by Bradley Hansell.

Prior to becoming Under Secretary, Moultrie's 36+ year career included serving in Senior Leadership positions throughout the DoD and the National Intelligence Community. He retired from the DoD in 2015 as NSA's Director of Operations. His previous National Intelligence Community roles included service as a senior member of the Central Intelligence Agency Senior Intelligence Service responsible for a major office within the Directorate of Science & Technology, and as a Senior Executive Officer serving the DNI. He later rejoined NSA as a Senior Executive, where he remained until retirement. He was also a Senior Advisor to the Secretary of the Navy, playing an instrumental role in the Department of the Navy Comprehensive Cybersecurity Review and the crafting of a digital roadmap for cybersecurity, data analytics and infrastructure, and emerging technologies including AI/Machine Learning, 5 and 6G, and Quantum computing.

Moultrie served as a Russian linguist in the United States Air Force ([`/organizations/us-air-force`]). He earned a Bachelor of Arts degree magna cum laude in Business Management from the University of Maryland, College Park ([`/organizations/umd`]), a Master of Science degree in Strategic Intelligence from the National Intelligence University ([`/organizations/national-intelligence-university`]), and a Russian Language degree from the Defense Language Institute ([`/organizations/defense-language-institute`]) at Monterey. He completed Senior Executive studies at Harvard University's Kennedy School of Government ([`/organizations/harvard-kennedy-school`]). He resides in Annapolis, Maryland with his wife Darlene.

## UAP Relevance

As Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security ([`/organizations/ousd-is`]), Moultrie was the principal advisor to the Secretary of Defense responsible for the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office ([`/organizations/aaro`]) at its establishment on July 20, 2022. He named Sean Kirkpatrick ([`/people/sean-kirkpatrick`]) as the office's first Director. On May 17, 2022, Moultrie appeared before Congress at the first open hearing on UAP in 50 years. On December 16, 2022, Moultrie appeared with Kirkpatrick at the AARO Media Roundtable, where he said he had 'not seen anything in those holdings to date that would suggest that there has been an alien visitation, an alien crash, or anything like that.'

Per DefenseScoop reporting (Brandi Vincent, August 30, 2023), between November 2022 and April 2023 the AARO website launch package moved back and forth between the OUSD(I&S) Front Office and AARO at least every other week at Moultrie's request; AARO regularly responded to questions, made edits and re-coordinated the memo, and Kirkpatrick had several in-person meetings with the undersecretary. In late July 2023 Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks ([`/people/kathleen-hicks`]) moved to personally oversee AARO and repositioned Kirkpatrick to report directly to her. Moultrie remained USD(I&S) until February 29, 2024.
