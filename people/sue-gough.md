## Background

Sue Gough is a civilian Department of Defense ([`/organizations/dod`]) spokesperson assigned to Defense Press Operations within the Office of the Secretary of Defense Public Affairs (OSD(PA)). Pentagon emails identify her as Gough, Susan L (Sue) CIV OSD PA (USA). Within OSD(PA) she is the principal Public Affairs contact for the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office ([`/organizations/aaro`]), providing on-record statements about AARO, its predecessor the UAP Task Force ([`/organizations/uaptf`]), and related Department of Defense matters.

She has held the AARO portfolio at OSD(PA) from June 2022 through February 2026 — an ongoing tenure across AARO's first Director Sean Kirkpatrick ([`/people/sean-kirkpatrick`]), Acting Director Tim Phillips ([`/people/tim-phillips`]), and Director Jon Kosloski ([`/people/jonathan-kosloski`]).

---

## UAP Relevance

Sue Gough is the principal Department of Defense Public Affairs contact for the AARO portfolio. She provided the June 29, 2022 Public Affairs response to The Black Vault ([`/organizations/the-black-vault`]) that named former Office of Naval Intelligence ([`/organizations/oni`]) senior civilian John Stratton ([`/people/jay-stratton`]) as UAPTF lead, noted Dr. Travis Taylor's ([`/people/travis-taylor`]) parent organization as the U.S. Army Space and Missile Defense Command ([`/organizations/u-s-army-smdc`]), and described Taylor as informally referred to chief scientist; was moderator of the December 16, 2022 joint AARO media engagement with Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence and Security Ronald Moultrie ([`/people/ronald-moultrie`]) and Director Sean Kirkpatrick (which described AARO as the office that replaced the former Navy-led ([`/organizations/us-navy`]) UAPTF and noted the UAPTF stands by the conclusions presented by Director Bray ([`/people/scott-bray`]) in his briefing); led OSD(PA) coordination on the AARO Historical Record Report Volume I press release rollout in March 2024 (FOIA case 24-F-0894); confirmed AARO Full Operational Capability as of Oct. 1, 2024 to DefenseScoop ([`/organizations/defensescoop`]) journalist Brandi Vincent ([`/people/brandi-vincent`]); and reported AARO's caseload of more than 2,000 UAP cases in February 2026.

> The Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force (UAPTF) was and is (until the follow-on Department of Defense [DOD] organization is fully operational) a partnership across federal and DOD agencies, with a variety of organizations providing personnel as required. Dr. Taylor was a government employee assigned to the U.S. Army Space and Missile Defense Command (SMDC). SMDC provided Dr. Taylor on a time-limited basis as part of a larger number of contributing organizations across the DOD, the Intelligence Community (IC), and other parts of the federal government to assist with the stand-up of the UAPTF and its reporting requirements. It was not a full time assignment; he remained an employee of SMDC. In early 2021, following consultations with the UAPTF, SMDC worked to clarify and de-conflict assigned tasks, responsibilities, and outside activities to balance mission priorities.
>

## Credibility Notes

Gough speaks for the Department of Defense as a civilian Public Affairs spokesperson, not in a personal capacity. Her on-record statements carry institutional Department of Defense positions; her internal Pentagon emails released in FOIA case 24-F-0894 ([`/documents/blackvault-foia-24-f-0894-aaro-vol-i-rollout-emails`]) document the operational coordination behind those positions. The release includes a March 5, 2024 OSD(PA)–ODNI ([`/organizations/odni`]) email exchange in which OSD(PA) and ODNI agree to the omission of any ODNI mention from the AARO Historical Record Report Volume I press release, even though Dir, AARO reports to PDDNI as well as the Deputy Secretary of Defense; an Importance: High email from Gough to OUSD(I&S) ([`/organizations/ousd-is`]) Director of Congressional Activities David Kozik ([`/people/david-kozik`]) on March 6, 2024 in which Gough states OSD(PA) does not want to see sUAS added to AARO's portfolio, sent in response to Senator Gillibrand ([`/people/kirsten-gillibrand`]) earlier the same day asking at a SASC briefing on the December 2023 JBLE drone incursions for sUAS to be catalogued by AARO; and a repeated email response from Gough to Scientific American, the Washington Examiner, and YLE Finland that declined further press engagement with Acting Director Tim Phillips ahead of an invite-only briefing on March 8, 2024.

In June 2025 the Wall Street Journal piece ([`/documents/wsj-pentagon-disinformation-ufo-mythology-2025`]) on Pentagon disinformation in UFO mythology — covered by tovima.com ([`/organizations/tovima`]) — noted on-record confirming from Gough that AARO had uncovered evidence of fake classified program materials, that the Department of Defense did not include those AARO findings in its Volume I report because the investigation was not yet completed, and that the Department was committed to releasing a second volume of its Historical Record Report to cover AARO's findings on reports of potential pranks and inauthentic materials later that year. As of February 25, 2026 (DefenseScoop), AARO had not released a 2025 annual report or Volume II.

Her email signature in the FOIA case 24-F-0894 emails read Gough, Susan L (Sue) CIV OSD PA (USA) — a civilian Office of the Secretary of Defense Public Affairs USA assignment. She has held the AARO portfolio across AARO's first Director Sean Kirkpatrick, Acting Director Tim Phillips, and Director Jon Kosloski, an ongoing tenure noted in sources from June 2022 through February 2026.
