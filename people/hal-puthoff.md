## Background

Harold E. Puthoff is a physicist. The To The Stars Academy of Arts & Science page (January 8, 2018) reports that Puthoff earned his Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1967 and that his professional background spans more than five decades of research at General Electric, Sperry, the National Security Agency ([`/organizations/nsa`]), Stanford University and SRI International, with patents issued in the laser, communications, and energy fields. The TTSA SEC Form 1-K Part II annual reports (May 2, 2022 and April 17, 2025 filings) state that Dr. Puthoff has served as President and CEO of EarthTech International, Inc. (ETI) and Director of the Institute for Advanced Studies at Austin (IASA) since 1985. The 1974 Nature paper 'Information transmission under conditions of sensory shielding' (Targ and Puthoff) describes experiments at the Electronics and Bioengineering Laboratory, Stanford Research Institute, Menlo Park, California. The 1977 CIA Studies in Intelligence paper by Kenneth A. Kress names Targ and Puthoff as the two laser physicists who re-awakened CIA research in parapsychology and describes Puthoff as a theoretician who was exploring new fields of research after extensive work in quantum electronics and who became interested in the field in the early 1970s.

## UAP Relevance

Puthoff is a co-founder, former Director, and current Advisory Board member of [`/organizations/ttsa`] To The Stars Academy of Arts & Science (per the TTSA SEC Form 1-A Part II preliminary offering circular dated July 10, 2017 and the TTSA SEC Form 1-K Part II annual reports filed May 2, 2022 and April 17, 2025). The TTSA announcement (July 26, 2018) of the ADAM Research Project (Acquisition and Data Analysis of Materials) names Dr. Harold E. Puthoff as 'a former Senior Advisor and Subcontractor to the Pentagon's AATIP program (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program) and current VP of Technology for TTS Academy', with EarthTech International, Inc. — under Puthoff's leadership — contracted to evaluate the material samples. The New York Times article 'Glowing Auras and Black Money: The Pentagon's Mysterious U.F.O. Program' (Helene Cooper, Ralph Blumenthal, and Leslie Kean, December 16, 2017) names Harold E. Puthoff as a contractor for the program who had earlier conducted research on extrasensory perception for the C.I.A., and reports that Elizondo joined Puthoff and Christopher K. Mellon in the new commercial venture called To the Stars Academy of Arts and Science. The TTSA press release on the ORNL Report (February 28, 2025) reports that Dr. Hal Puthoff and Dr. Eric Davis's analysis of the Magnesium-Zinc-Bismuth (MgZn/Bi) 'Art's Parts' sample was completed and submitted to the TTSA Board of Directors on the 27th of September 2024. Puthoff's SRI institutional context is documented by the 1977 Kress paper, the 1993 DIA STAR GATE Project Overview (which describes the SRI International program at Menlo Park from 1970 to 1990 as a small group headed by Dr. H. Puthoff), and the 1995 American Institutes for Research evaluation (which reviews the 1973-1988 SRI experiments and cites Puthoff and Targ (1975) 'Perceptual Augmentation Techniques: Part Two — Research Report' as the SRI Final Report).

## Credibility Notes

Puthoff's professional record is documented across primary sources spanning more than five decades. The 1977 CIA Studies in Intelligence paper by Kenneth A. Kress ([`/people/kenneth-kress`]) — declassified June 23, 2011 (CIA-RDP88G01116R000600690003-8) — names Targ and Puthoff as the two laser physicists who re-awakened CIA ([`/organizations/cia`]) research in parapsychology, describes Puthoff as a theoretician who was exploring new fields of research after extensive work in quantum electronics, and documents the August 1972 Office of Technical Service contract with Targ and Puthoff at SRI ($874 for a few days of work with the magnetometer subject), the October 1, 1972 OTS Contract 8473 expanded effort, the February 1, 1974 jointly funded ORD and OTS program at SRI, and the November 1976 briefing of Director George Bush on CIA's investigations into parapsychology after he became aware that official Soviets were visiting and questioning Puthoff and Targ at SRI. The 1993 DIA ([`/organizations/dia`]) STAR GATE Project Overview describes the SRI International program at Menlo Park as a small group headed by Dr. H. Puthoff and reports the program discontinued in 1990. The 1995 American Institutes for Research ([`/organizations/american-institutes-for-research`]) evaluation reviews the 1973-1988 SRI experiments and cites Puthoff, Harold E. and Russell Targ (1975) 'Perceptual Augmentation Techniques: Part Two — Research Report' as the SRI Final Report (December 1, 1975).

The TTSA page (January 8, 2018) is the most comprehensive single source on Puthoff's career: it states that Puthoff earned his Ph.D. from Stanford University in 1967, that his professional background spans more than five decades of research at General Electric, Sperry, the National Security Agency, Stanford University and SRI International, that he has served as President and CEO of EarthTech International, Inc. (ETI) and Director of the Institute for Advanced Studies at Austin (IASA) since 1985, that he holds patents issued in the laser, communications, and energy fields, and that he won a Who's Who Lifetime Achievement in 2017. The TTSA SEC Form 1-A Part II (July 10, 2017) and SEC Form 1-K Part II (filed May 2, 2022 and April 17, 2025) corroborate the EarthTech President and IASA Director roles since 1985 and the TTSA co-founder role; the May 2, 2022 filing reports that Puthoff recently left his position on the Company's board of directors to serve on the Advisory Board, and the April 17, 2025 filing records the September 27, 2024 Puthoff/Davis memorandum to the Board of Directors on the ORNL ([`/organizations/ornl`]) Art's Parts material analysis. The December 16, 2017 New York Times article describes Puthoff as 'an engineer who has conducted research on extrasensory perception for the C.I.A. and later worked as a contractor for the program'; other primary sources describe Puthoff as a physicist (Kress 1977: 'Two lasqr physicists, Dr. Russell Targ and Dr. Harold E.
