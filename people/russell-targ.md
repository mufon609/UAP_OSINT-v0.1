## Background

Russell Targ is a physicist and author. Per the IRVA page, Targ has a bachelor's degree in physics from Queens College and did graduate work in physics at Columbia University. He has received two National Aeronautics and Space-Administration awards for inventions and contributions to lasers and laser communications. Per Targ's ESP Research site, Targ was a pioneer in the development of the laser and cofounder of the Stanford Research Institute's investigation into psychic abilities in the 1970s and 1980s. The 1974 Nature paper 'Information transmission under conditions of sensory shielding' (Targ and Puthoff) reports experiments at the Electronics and Bioengineering Laboratory, Stanford Research Institute, Menlo Park, California. Applied Optics author affiliations show Lockheed Missiles & Space Company (1991) and Advanced Technology Center, Lockheed Martin (1996), both at 3251 Hanover Street, Palo Alto, California. Per the IRVA page, Targ presented remote-viewing demonstrations to the USSR Academy of Sciences in 1983 and 1984, and retired from Lockheed Martin in 1997 after he developed airborne laser systems for the detection of windshear and air turbulence.

---

## UAP Relevance

Targ is the cofounder of the Stanford Research Institute investigation into psychic abilities in the 1970s and 1980s — the institutional context for the remote viewing research that the 1977 CIA Kress paper, the 1993 DIA STAR GATE Project Overview, and the 1995 American Institutes for Research evaluation document at SRI International, Menlo Park. The Kress paper places Targ's April 1972 meeting with the CIA Office of Scientific Intelligence as the first documented CIA contact and the August 1972 Office of Technical Service contract with Targ and Puthoff at SRI as the first contract funded by CIA. The STAR GATE Project Overview describes the SRI program at Menlo Park from 1970 to 1990 with CIA funding to SRI for continuing remote viewing research, discontinued in 1975, and subsequent funding provided by DIA. The AIR evaluation reviews the 1973-1988 SRI experiments and includes Puthoff and Targ (1975) "Perceptual Augmentation Techniques: Part Two — Research Report" — the Stanford Research Institute Final Report. Targ's Lockheed Martin senior staff scientist role places him at the Palo Alto site at 3251 Hanover Street; he retired in 1997 after work on airborne laser systems for windshear and air turbulence detection.

## Credibility Notes

Targ's ESP Research site (his own publication) names him as a pioneer in the development of the laser and cofounder of the Stanford Research Institute investigation into psychic abilities.
