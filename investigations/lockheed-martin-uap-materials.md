# Lockheed Martin — UAP Materials

## Question

Does Lockheed Martin own or house UAP material?

---

## Description

This investigation evaluates whether Lockheed Martin ([`/organizations/lockheed-martin`]) owns or houses UAP material against the primary-source record. The question is on the public record through a multi-source allegation chain naming Lockheed Martin Space Systems Vice President Dr. James Ryder ([`/people/james-ryder`]) as having proposed a 2011 divestment of recovered material to Bigelow Aerospace ([`/organizations/bigelow-aerospace`]) under an AAWSAP-PSAP ([`/organizations/aawsap`]) Technology Transfer Agreement. The chain spans James Lacatski's ([`/people/james-lacatski`]) SD004 page-1 statement (DOPSR-cleared), Luis Elizondo's ([`/people/luis-elizondo`]) QFR Q7 response (signed congressional record), David Grusch's ([`/people/david-grusch`]) on-camera attestation that he provided Lockheed Martin to the ICIG (Joe Rogan Experience), George Knapp's ([`/people/george-knapp`]) sworn 2025-09-09 House Oversight testimony naming Ryder at Lockheed, and Christopher Sharp's ([`/people/christopher-sharp`]) Liberation Times reporting across 2023-2026. The alleged transfer was blocked by Glenn Gaffney ([`/people/glenn-gaffney`]) at the CIA Directorate of Science & Technology and ultimately by Deputy DNI Robert Cardillo ([`/people/robert-cardillo`]) and Deputy SecDef William Lynn III ([`/people/william-lynn-iii`]) per Sharp's reporting. AARO's ([`/organizations/aaro`]) Historical Record Report Volume I institutional finding states "AARO found no empirical evidence for claims that the USG and private companies have been reverse-engineering extraterrestrial technology" — a current-executive on-record denial that does not reach the alleged 2011 timeframe. Lockheed Martin has documented a consistent refusal to issue categorical denials across multiple journalistic venues over sixteen months. F2 ([`/findings/pax-river-scif-fiscal-triangulation`]) grounds the alleged transfer-receiving Patuxent River SAP-F hangar through three-source fiscal-data convergence (Steny Hoyer's ([`/people/steny-hoyer`]) 2010 Fiscal Year 2011 Military Construction appropriation; Elizondo's 2024 QFR Q7; Liberation Times reporting on Rep. Eric Burlison's ([`/people/eric-burlison`]) February 2026 White House-approved site visit). The investigation consumes the F.7 findings layer to evaluate four competing hypotheses against the structural state of the primary-source record.

## Hypotheses

- **H1** — Lockheed Martin custodied UAP material as of ~2011 and attempted divestment to Bigelow Aerospace under an AAWSAP Technology Transfer Agreement, blocked by the CIA Directorate of Science & Technology and ultimately by the Deputy Director of National Intelligence and the Deputy Secretary of Defense.
- **H2** — Lockheed Martin currently houses UAP material (post-2011, through the time of writing).
- **H3** — The Patuxent River SAP-F hangar (FY2011 appropriation, approximately $10 million, requested by then-Representative Steny Hoyer) was purpose-built to receive the proposed Lockheed-Bigelow material transfer.
- **H0** — The Lockheed Martin / Bigelow Aerospace transfer allegation chain is fabricated, mistaken, or a coordinated information operation across independent witnesses.

## Hypothesis Evaluation

### H1 — Lockheed Martin custodied UAP material as of ~2011 and attempted divestment to Bigelow Aerospace under an AAWSAP Technology Transfer Agreement, blocked by the CIA Directorate of Science & Technology and ultimately by the Deputy Director of National Intelligence and the Deputy Secretary of Defense.

**Status:** Substantiated as allegation on record; not established as physical fact.

The primary-source record converges across independent sources naming Lockheed Martin as a 2011 custodian with an attempted divestment to Bigelow Aerospace via the AAWSAP-PSAP route, blocked by the CIA Directorate of Science & Technology under Glenn Gaffney and ultimately by Deputy DNI Robert Cardillo and Deputy SecDef William Lynn III. The chain spans multiple evidentiary tiers: Lacatski's SD004 page-1 statement (DOPSR-cleared 2023-09-06), Elizondo's QFR Q7 response to Burlison (signed congressional record, 2024-12-19), Grusch's on-camera attestation that he provided Lockheed Martin specifically to the ICIG (Joe Rogan Experience 2023-11-21), Knapp's sworn 2025-09-09 House Oversight testimony naming Ryder at Lockheed, and Christopher Sharp's Liberation Times reporting across 2023-2026. Independence across witness types — DIA program leadership (Lacatski), DoD/AAWSAP (Elizondo), NGA/UAPTF (Grusch), investigative journalism (Knapp, Sharp) — distinguishes the convergence from a coordinated information operation. F2 (Pax River fiscal-data triangulation) provides structural corroboration: Hoyer's 2010 appropriation predates the UAP framing by fourteen years and was a routine Military Construction announcement at the time. The record does not establish that the proposed transfer actually involved physical material; it establishes that the allegation is on record at multiple primary-source tiers.

### H2 — Lockheed Martin currently houses UAP material (post-2011, through the time of writing).

**Status:** Not established by primary sources; non-denial pattern is suggestive but not affirmative.

No primary source attests Lockheed Martin currently holds UAP material in the post-2011 timeframe. The strongest indirect signal is the documented pattern of Lockheed Martin refusing to issue categorical denials across multiple journalistic venues — Sharp reports the company "refused on multiple occasions to comment" (2024-11-08) and that a Lockheed Martin spokesperson responded to a direct November 2023 query about possessing materials of unknown or non-human origin with "Questions about UAPs are best addressed by the U.S. government" rather than a denial. The pattern is consistent with either continued custody or corporate-policy non-engagement with UAP allegations; primary sources do not distinguish between these readings. AARO's Historical Record Report Volume I institutional finding addresses a different action — "ever recovered, possessed, or engaged in reverse-engineering" — than the allegation describes (a 2011 disapproved-transfer-proposal), and rests on current-executive on-record statements that do not reach the alleged 2011-or-earlier custody chain. The denial publication (2024-03-08) also predates Sharp's subsequent documentation of Lockheed Martin's own non-denial responses to the same allegation cluster.

### H3 — The Patuxent River SAP-F hangar (FY2011 appropriation, approximately $10 million, requested by then-Representative Steny Hoyer) was purpose-built to receive the proposed Lockheed-Bigelow material transfer.
