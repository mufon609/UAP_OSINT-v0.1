SCOPE OF THIS SIBLING
=====================

This `.txt` sibling covers PAGE 1 ONLY of the 21-page SD004 PDF —
the load-bearing contemporary Q&A content authored by an unnamed
contributor under DoD prepublication review (DOPSR) clearance dated
2023-09-06.

Pages 2-21 of the SD004 PDF are appended historical primary-source
attachments from the National Archives — each a famous UAP-related
government document with its own provenance and worthy of dedicated
document-node treatment. Specifically:

- pp. 2-3: 25 May 1950 USAF OSI memo from Doyle Rees on aerial
  phenomena in the New Mexico area (Dec 1948 - May 1950).
- pp. 4-6: 23 Sep 1947 Air Materiel Command "AMC Opinion Concerning
  Flying Discs" memo, Lt Gen Nathan F. Twining to Brig Gen George
  Schulgen.
- pp. 7-12: 24 Sep 1952 / 2 Oct 1952 H. Marshall Chadwell CIA Office
  of Scientific Intelligence "Flying Saucers" memorandum to the
  Director of Central Intelligence.
- pp. 13-15: 12 May 1952 FBI Savannah teletype + 25 May 1952 J. Edgar
  Hoover memo on Savannah River Plant disc sightings.
- p. 16: 18 Oct 1950 FBI Knoxville memo on radar detection of
  unidentified objects over Oak Ridge, Oct 12-16, 1950.
- pp. 17-18: 15 Oct 1950 / 16 Oct 1950 / 20 Oct 1950 OSI / Atomic
  Energy Commission Security Patrol incident reports — Oak Ridge area.
- p. 19: 1949 Air Defense Command incident reports.
- pp. 20-21: 23 Sep 1957 FBI Belmont memo on a 20 Sep 1957
  unidentified flying object reported by U.S. Air Defense Command
  (Montauk Point / Benton, Pennsylvania radar tracking).

Each appended primary source carries its own classification chain,
authorship, dates, and historical significance independent of
SD004's contemporary Q&A content. Future contributors building those
document nodes should produce their own `.txt` siblings (or, if the
primary sources are independently archived elsewhere in the
public record, register the cleaner originals as separate manifest
entries) rather than extending this file. This sibling exists for
the page-1 contributor Q&A only.

EXTRACTION METHODOLOGY
======================

The SD004 PDF has no embedded text layer (verified: pdftotext output
is zero bytes). All 21 pages are 200-DPI scanned images embedded as
JPEG (color) or CCITT (grayscale). The page-1 transcription below
was produced by visually reading the page-1 image via a multimodal
LLM and authoring the verbatim text from what is legibly displayed.
Bracketed annotations [in square brackets] document where DOPSR /
disclaimer text overlays partially occlude the underlying Q&A text.

Q2: [partially obscured] intent of the $22M allocated by Sen Harry Reid for the DIA Program (AAWSAP)?
----------------------------------------------------------------

Open source ref: https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2021/05/10/how-the-pentagon-started-taking-ufos-seriously (Reid confirms Lockheed Martin)

https://www.dia.mil/FOIA/FOIA-Electronic-Reading-Room/FileId/170015/  (Sen Reid SAP Request)

Pages 152-153 "Skinwalkers at the Pentagon" (DOPSR Approved)

The $22M allocated for DIA AAWSAP, after speaking to multiple
individuals on the program, was originally intended to SCIF-out
Bigelow Aerospace Facilities in Las Vegas due to a UAP material
divestment plan proposed to AAWSAP leadership by Lockheed Martin
Space Systems Vice President Dr. James Ryder (now deceased). A
specific facility known to me was described as having crash
retrieval material from the 1950s and other historical operations.
They proposed a technology transfer agreement (TTA) into the
propose Waived PSAP Sen Reid was championing (See DIA FOIA
release). Unfortunately, due to the sensitive nature of the TTA,
USDI and OSD leadership were not specifically briefed on this
proposal. This is why, in "Skinwalkers at the Pentagon," they
approach DHS S&T, Tara O'Toole to set up a PSAP. Ultimately the
original government custodian, CIA DS&T, disapproved that transfer
during a meeting with Glenn Gaffney while he was in that position.
Instead, much of the money was used to produce Defense Intelligence
Reference Documents (DIRDs) to possibly show value to the Legacy
Program.
