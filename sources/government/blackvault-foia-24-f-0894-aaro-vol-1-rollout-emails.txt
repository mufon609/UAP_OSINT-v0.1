Thanks, Heather. Copying below the quick read-out of the AARO briefings from our team in case helpful:

Mr. Tim Phillips, Acting Director AARO, and (b)(6) were joined by Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick in a compartmented brief and a TS//SCI brief to staff on the findings of the Historical Record Report

BLUF: Both briefs were well attended and went well. Dr. Kirkpatrick attended the first brief and the majority of the second brief. Questions were robust in both sessions and the staff seemed appreciative of the information and AARO's work. There are a few due outs.

Session 1: Compartmented Annex Brief to Senior Staff; 1300-1400

The session was well attended. Staff Directors and Clerks from all six intelligence and defense committees attended, along with Senate Leadership National Security Advisors (House Leadership NSA's were invited but did not attend). Mr. Phillips delivered prepared opening remarks to convey the key takeaways from the report, before inviting questions from the attendees. Although it seemed most in the room had not had the chance to read the annex in advance of the session, the discussion was robust. Staff asked numerous questions about the individuals AARO interviewed as part of the review, and some of the noteworthy programs to include KONA BLUE. Staff also asked questions to better understand AARO perspectives on risk and CI implications of the findings in the report. DoD SAPCO provided copies of the annex for reference during the brief and Mr. Phillips, Dr. Kirkpatrick, and (b)(6) highlighted different sections during the Q&A session to provide context and additional detail. At the end of the session, staff asked for perspectives and recommendations for countering inaccurate or misleading narratives related to UAP based on the review. The staff were appreciative of the information and thanked the panel for the time.

Session 2: TS//SCI Brief to Intelligence and Defense Committee PSMs; 1400-1530

This session was also well attended. 24 PSMs representing all six of the invited committees joined the session. SSCI Minority Staff Director Brian Walsh stayed for the second session. Mr. Phillips again delivered his opening remarks and presented from prepared slides to convey the key findings from the report and provide amplifying detail. Staff began asking questions early on and Mr. Phillips, Dr. Kirkpatrick, and (b)(6) fielded questions as they came up, referring to the slides as needed. PSM questions varied, but recurring themes centered on the number of total people interviewed versus contacted over the course of the review and the supporting documentation, locations visited as part of the review, and plans for future UAPs. Staff also asked numerous questions about the programs highlighted during the presentation to include KONA BLUE. At the end of the session, PSMs thanked the panel for their time and the information.
