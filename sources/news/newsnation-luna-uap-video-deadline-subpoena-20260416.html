Pentagon misses UAP video deadline. Rep. Luna eyes subpoena

Robert Jones

Updated: Apr 17, 2026 / 11:39 PM CDT

WASHINGTON (NewsNation) — A House lawmaker is threatening subpoena action after the Pentagon failed to meet a congressional deadline to turn over dozens of classified UAP videos, saying she is prepared to compel production of the footage if “institutional resistance continues.”

Rep. Anna Paulina Luna, R-Fla., chairwoman of the House Oversight Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, told NewsNation Thursday that the Department of War missed her April 14 deadline to deliver 46 UAP video files she formally requested in a March 31 letter to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth.

Luna said she intends to work directly with Hegseth to secure the footage and described him as a close working partner aligned with President Donald Trump’s directive on UAP disclosure. She said she is confident cooperation between her office and Pentagon leadership can produce the requested materials.

However, Luna warned that if resistance continues, she is prepared to work with House Oversight Committee Chairman James Comer to exercise the committee’s subpoena authority.

She also called for the removal of the unelected official she said was responsible for failing to route her letter to the appropriate authorities within the department.

The 46 videos requested in Luna’s March 31 letter span multiple military branches and locations and include footage of UAP formations captured over Iran, the Persian Gulf, the East China Sea and near U.S. domestic airports.

Among the files requested is footage of an F-16 shooting down a UAP over Lake Huron in February 2023 and multiple incidents documented by MQ-9 drone operators.

Luna’s task force has been investigating UAP disclosure since at least September 2025, when it held a hearing during which whistleblowers testified that the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office, known as AARO, possesses additional video records of potential UAP sightings that have not been publicly released.

“The lack of disclosure regarding the very real threat posed by UAPs in and around U.S. restricted airspace is concerning,” Luna wrote in her March 31 letter. “The presence of UAPs in and around the sensitive airspaces of U.S. military installations poses a threat to the security of the armed forces and their readiness.”

Luna has also faulted AARO directly, saying the office’s responses when questioned about UAP sightings have been “less than adequate.”

The House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform holds broad investigative authority under House Rule X to examine “any matter” at “any time.”
